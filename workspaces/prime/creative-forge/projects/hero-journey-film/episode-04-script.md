# 🎬 MOVIE 1: "THE HERO'S JOURNEY"
## Episode 4: "The Darkest Hour" (~3.5 min, 18 scenes)
_2023. The shark. The cash crisis. Fernando breaking. LBI. The bottom of the circle._

---

### SCENE 1 — THE NUMBERS (10s)
_[MUSIC: Low. Grinding. The sound of something being crushed slowly.]_

(Carol Kern the Owl's office. She's surrounded by spreadsheets. Red numbers EVERYWHERE. Her calculator is SMOKING.)

CAROL: (on phone, barely holding it together) Tom. TOM. The filing fees are running over a million dollars a MONTH. The No Surprises Act decisions aren't coming out. No decisions means no cash flow. We are HEMORRHAGING.

TOM: (on the other end, cigar trembling) How long do we have?

CAROL: (looking at her screen) Months. Maybe. If nothing changes.

(Silence on the line. Two animals who've spent their careers building something, watching the foundation crack.)

### SCENE 2 — THE SHARK ARRIVES (10s)
_[MUSIC: A new theme. Smooth jazz. TOO smooth. Something's wrong.]_

(A conference room. A cartoon GREAT WHITE SHARK glides in wearing a designer suit. Smile full of teeth. Handshake that doesn't let go.)

SHARK: (spreading papers across the table) Gentlemen. I'm here to HELP. Financing. Partnership. Growth capital. Everything you need to get through this rough patch.

(Sean, Tom, and Mark sit across from him.)

SEAN: What's the structure?

SHARK: (waving his fin dismissively) Details, details. The POINT is — I believe in what you're building. Let me be part of it.

(His smile never wavers. His eyes never blink. Tom's cigar smoke curls between them like a warning.)

### SCENE 3 — THE VALUATION (10s)
(Later. Sean and Tom reviewing the shark's proposal.)

TOM: (reading, slower and slower) He's valuing the company at... THIS?

SEAN: (leaning in) What?

TOM: It's massively overvalued. On paper it looks like he's giving us everything. But in the fine print...

(Tom flips pages.)

TOM: He keeps all the benefits. Controls the board. And his actual cash contribution is...

(Long pause.)

TOM: Zero. He's putting in NOTHING.

SEAN: (sitting back) He's trying to steal it. With a smile and a handshake.

TOM: (cigar, quiet fury) He's trying to steal it with a HUG.

### SCENE 4 — THE SHARK'S TRUE FACE (5s)
(Quick shot: The shark on his phone in a parking lot after the meeting. His smile drops. Cold calculation replaces warmth.)

SHARK: (into phone) They're desperate. The filing fees are crushing them. No decisions coming out. Give it two more months and I'll have the whole thing for nothing.

(He hangs up. The smile returns. He straightens his tie.)

### SCENE 5 — MARK AND SEAN — FORCED TOGETHER (10s)
(The irony: the crisis FORCES Mark and Sean to work together. They're in the same foxhole now whether they like it or not.)

MARK: (looking at the same spreadsheet) If we don't find real financing — not that shark — we're done.

SEAN: (exhausted) I know.

MARK: (for the first time, no eye roll) What do you need from me?

(Sean looks at Mark. Really looks. Through the frustration, through the history, through every eye roll — Mark just asked the right question.)

SEAN: (quiet) I need you to stop seeing me as the problem.

MARK: (long pause, then honestly) I never thought you were the problem, Sean. I thought the DREAM was the problem. But I'm starting to think... maybe I was wrong about which one was real.

### SCENE 6 — FERNANDO AT THE EDGE (10s)
_[MUSIC: Strings. Pulling. Something about to snap.]_

(Fernando the Fox. Alone in his apartment. Phone dark. He's sitting in a chair staring at nothing. The red leather jacket hangs in the closet behind him. He doesn't reach for it.)

FERNANDO: (to himself) I gave up everything for this. My career. My comfort. Years.

(He looks at the closet.)

FERNANDO: And I don't know if I have another year in me.

(His phone lights up. A text from Sean: "Can you come to LBI? I need to talk. All of us.")

(Fernando stares at it for a long time.)

### SCENE 7 — LONG BEACH ISLAND (10s)
_[MUSIC: Ocean. Waves. Something ancient and honest.]_

(Long Beach Island. Sean's beach house. Sunset. Four animals sit in a circle on the porch. Sean the Lion. Fernando the Fox. Adam the Bear. Tink the Fairy — her glow dimmer than usual.)

(Nobody speaks for a moment. The ocean fills the silence.)

SEAN: (finally, raw) I'm going to say things I've never said out loud.

(They wait.)

### SCENE 8 — THE TRUTH (10s)
SEAN: I am exhausted. Not tired — EXHAUSTED. My heart is broken. The people I built everything for are fighting everything I'm building. The money is running out. A shark is circling. And the person I trusted to help me LEAD this—

(He looks at Fernando.)

SEAN: —I can feel slipping away.

(Fernando's ears flatten. He doesn't deny it.)

SEAN: So here's what I need to say. If we're going to survive this, I need a new and different world of commitment. From all of us. Not the same commitment. A DIFFERENT one.

### SCENE 9 — FERNANDO SPEAKS (10s)
(Fernando doesn't look at Sean. He looks at the ocean.)

FERNANDO: (voice tight) You know what I gave up to be here. You KNOW.

SEAN: I do.

FERNANDO: And I would do it again. Every time. But Sean... I need you to see what I'm carrying. Not just what YOU'RE carrying.

(Adam puts a paw on Fernando's shoulder. Not fixing. Just present.)

ADAM: (with love) We all see it, Fernando.

(Tink sprinkles pixie dust that catches the sunset light.)

TINK: (soft) We're still here. That's not nothing.

### SCENE 10 — THE COMMITMENT (10s)
_[MUSIC: A shift. The minor key finds a major note. Not resolved. But turning.]_

(They talk until the stars come out. We don't hear all of it. We see the shapes of it — hands gesturing, heads nodding, one moment of laughter that breaks the tension.)

(Finally: all four of them standing. Facing the ocean.)

SEAN: 2024. Unblinded moves differently. I don't know how yet. But differently.

FERNANDO: (quiet, certain) Differently.

ADAM: (arm around both) Differently.

(Tink flies up. Her glow brightens. Just a little.)

### SCENE 11 — CAROL'S DARKEST MOMENT (5s)
(Meanwhile: Carol Kern alone in her office. The numbers haven't changed. She's staring at a screen that shows a countdown to insolvency.)

CAROL: (whispering to her calculator) Please. Just one decision. Just ONE.

### SCENE 12 — THE SNAKE'S LAST POISON (10s)
(The office manager snake. Still there. Still smiling. Still dropping poison.)

OFFICE MANAGER SNAKE: (in the hallway, to a group of employees) I just think everyone should know... the filing fees are in the millions now. And those Tony Robbins trips? They're not paying for themselves. Just... be prepared. That's all I'm saying.

(She smiles sweetly. Walks away. Leaves a trail of doubt behind her like a chemical spill.)

(One employee looks at another.)

EMPLOYEE: Do you think she's right?

EMPLOYEE 2: I think she's always right. And I think that's the problem.

### SCENE 13 — THE NO SURPRISES ACT — NOTHING (5s)
(A courthouse. EMPTY. Closed doors. A sign: "DECISIONS PENDING.")

(Sean on the phone.)

SEAN: Any update on the TMA cases?

VOICE: Nothing yet. Could be months.

(Sean hangs up. The ocean from LBI feels very far away.)

### SCENE 14 — SEAN AT THE BOTTOM (10s)
_[MUSIC: The lowest point. Almost silent. A single cello.]_

(Sean's home office. 2 AM. He's not working. He's just sitting. His sunglasses are on the desk — for the first time, we see his eyes. Tired. Hurt. But not defeated.)

(He picks up a framed photo. His team at Grand Cayman 2017. Everyone smiling. Everyone believing.)

SEAN: (to the photo) I promised you. All of you. That this would mean something.

(He sets it down carefully.)

SEAN: It still will. I just need... one more door to open.

### SCENE 15 — THE PRAYER (5s)
(Different place. Different animal. Carol Kern the Owl, in a church. Alone in a pew. Calculator on the seat next to her. Even she needs more than numbers right now.)

(Mark the Deer, in his car in the parking lot. Engine off. Staring at the steering wheel. Not going in. Not driving away.)

(Fernando, on a rooftop. Looking at the skyline. The red leather jacket back on. For comfort this time, not vanity.)

### SCENE 16 — THE CRACK OF DAWN (10s)
_[MUSIC: A single note. Then another. Then another. Building like a sunrise.]_

(January 2024. A phone rings. Sean answers.)

VOICE: Sean? The TMA decisions. They're coming in.

(Sean doesn't move. Doesn't breathe.)

VOICE: Texas Medical Association versus CMS. In favor. Across the board. Filing fees restructured. Decisions starting to flow.

(A single tear tracks down Sean's face. The sunglasses are still off.)

SEAN: (barely a whisper) Say that again.

VOICE: You heard me, Sean. The seas are parting.

### SCENE 17 — END CARD (5s)
_[MUSIC: The sunrise builds. Not triumphant yet. But LIGHT.]_

(Sean's face. Eyes we've never seen before. Wet. Bright. Alive.)

(Text:)

"The TMA decisions changed everything."
"The seas parted."
"And on the other side of the darkest hour..."

### SCENE 18 — FLASH (5s)
(Quick image: a golden lightning bolt CRACKING through a dark sky.)

(Text:)

"...was a miracle nobody saw coming."

"Except one blind lion."
