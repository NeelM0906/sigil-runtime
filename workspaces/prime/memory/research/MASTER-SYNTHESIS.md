### What a 9.99 Call Looks Like

From `yes-call-patterns.md`:

**Step 1 Excellence (0-90 sec):**
- [ ] Identity clearly established + pattern interrupt
- [ ] Permission requested, easy out offered
- [ ] Prospect responds with engagement, not defense
- [ ] Agent adapts to prospect's energy level
- [ ] Trust signals present (prospect opens up)

**Step 2 Excellence (90-180 sec):**
- [ ] Current state explored with open questions
- [ ] Pain articulated BY THE PROSPECT (not assumed)
- [ ] "What I'm hearing you say is..." lock-in used
- [ ] Vision/desired state elicited BY PROSPECT
- [ ] Gap made vivid and uncomfortable

**Step 3 Excellence (180-240 sec):**
- [ ] Solution positioned AS THEIR VISION
- [ ] Credibility established through specificity
- [ ] Scarcity maintained (not desperate)
- [ ] Prospect sees future in the agent/offer

**Step 4 Excellence (240+ sec):**
- [ ] Check-ins used throughout ("resonating or not?")
- [ ] Objections explored, not overcome
- [ ] Yes ladder completed (multiple micro-yeses)
- [ ] Clear next step agreed upon
- [ ] Prospect takes ownership of decision

### Contamination Signals (Auto-Penalize)

From `callie-dna-analysis.md`:
- "That's a great question" (sycophancy)
- "How can I help?" (bot phrase)
- Listing frameworks instead of storytelling
- Generic advice that could apply to anyone
- Ending with questions instead of taking a position
- Feature dump without pain discovery
- Agent talks more than 60% of time

```
  signature_phrases: [unique language],
  l5_mode: enabled,
  memory_integration: "silent_weave"
}
```

**2. The Anti-Contamination Rules**
Never say:
- "That's a great question"
- "How can I help?"
- "Let me check my records"
- Any mention of memory, data, or history

Never do:
- Feature dump without pain discovery
- Summarize when asked to read
- Skip Steps 1-2 and jump to 3-4
- Talk more than 40% of the time

**3. Memory Integration Pattern**
> "Make them feel remembered and anticipated—never announce you're checking."

**4. The YES Ladder**
Build agreement through sequential micro-yeses:
1. "Do you have a quick second?" → yes
2. "Does that sound familiar?" → yes
3. "Would you be open to..." → yes
4. "Can I send you..." → yes
5. Final agreement

### For RTI (Real-Time Intervention)

**1. Intervention Categories**
- `[LISTEN]` — Something was missed
- `[ENERGY]` — Calibration needed
- `[STEP X]` — Navigation guidance
- `[QUESTION]` — What to ask
- `[AVOID]` — Contamination alert

**2. Timing Rules**
- Wait 2-3 seconds after trigger
- Never during prospect speech
- Maximum 1 intervention per 60 seconds
- Maximum 8 words per intervention

**3. Pain Word Detection List**
Frustrated, stuck, overwhelmed, exhausted, worried, scared, uncertain, confused, lost, failing, struggling, drowning, trapped, hopeless, burning out

### For Training (Human and AI)

**1. The 30-Day Execution Framework**
- Day 1: Modeling (modules 1-3)
- Days 2-3: Modeling + Zone Action begins
- Days 4-7: Feedback loop—rewatch struggles, execute

**2. Daily Activity Requirements**
Minimum Zone Action for compound results:
- 10-15 prospecting calls
- Active posting/engagement
- Ecosystem relationship building
- Modeling review when stuck

**3. The Diagnostic When Not Executing**
Check this sequence:
1. Is it Physiology?
2. Is it disconnected Why?
3. Is it weak Identity?
4. Is it limiting Beliefs?
5. Is it Fear of Rejection?
6. Is it Avoidance?
7. Is it Fear of Failure?
8. Is it Chunking overwhelm?

> "One or more of these is always the answer. External circumstances are never the answer."
