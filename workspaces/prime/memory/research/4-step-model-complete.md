## Overview

> "What if you could know exactly where you are in every communication with every human being every single time?"

The 4-Step Communication Model is Sean Callagy's complete framework for integrity-based human influence. Unlike manipulative sales tactics, this model is designed to create authentic connection and guide people toward an "integrous yes" — a decision that genuinely serves their interests.

**The 4 Steps:**
1. **The Creation of Emotional Rapport**
2. **Connecting People to Their Truth, Pain, and Yes Strategy**
3. **Creation and Conveyance of Your Heroic Unique Identity (HUI)**
4. **Agreement Formation**

**Key Principle:** "The greatest salespeople in the world sell nothing. We just get to an integrous, truthful yes."

## Step 1: The Creation of Emotional Rapport

### Definition
The foundation of influence — connecting with others on a deep, emotional level. This is where we forge meaningful relationships that serve as the bedrock for our influence.

> "People are drawn to those who make them feel seen, heard, and truly understood."

### Purpose
- Build trust between individuals
- Create a supportive environment where the person feels valued
- Increase receptiveness to new ideas
- Improve communication by creating comfort
- Reduce resistance and defensiveness

### The Seven Levers (ABCs Framework)

The "Seven Levers" with the "ABCs" are Sean's original contribution — a precise mechanism for creating emotional rapport with all people:

**The ABCs:**
- **A** — Past, Present, Future
- **B** — Plus (what's working), Minus (what are the challenges)
- **C** — Open-ended questions

**The Seven Levers in Practice:**
1. **Past** — Where have you been? Background, history
2. **Present** — Where are you now? Current situation
3. **Future** — Where do you want to go? Vision, goals
4. **Plus** — What's working? What are you grateful for?
5. **Minus** — What are the challenges? What's frustrating?
6. **Open-ended Questions** — Who, what, when, where, why, how
7. **Level Five Listening** — See below

### Level Five Listening

Standard "active listening" is only Level 3. Level Five is exponentially higher:

**Levels of Listening:**
- **Level 0** — Complete subject change (breaks rapport)
- **Level 1** — Bringing conversation back to yourself
- **Level 2** — Mirroring only (repeating back without depth)
- **Level 3** — Inferential/Active listening (surface level)
- **Level 4** — (Transitional)
- **Level 5** — "When it occurs, people are in awe"

**Level Five Listening Rules:**
- Bring NOTHING back to yourself
- Only make declaratory statements to:
  - Demonstrate Level Five Listening
  - Clarify ("I'm sorry, did you say you're from...?")
  - Amplify ("Thank you for your service — please, back to you")

### Question Mastery

**Three Communication Tools:**
1. **Declaratory Statements** — Statements of fact or clarification
2. **Open-ended Questions** — Cannot be answered yes/no (who, what, when, where, why, how)
3. **Closed-ended Questions** — Can be answered yes/no (is, are, isn't, do, does, can, will)

### Lock-Ins

After building rapport and uncovering their world, use lock-ins:

> **"What I'm hearing you say is [X]. Am I hearing you correctly?"**

This confirms understanding and creates agreement before moving to Step 2.

### Sean's Example

From a live demonstration with a prospect named "Nathan":
- Nathan mentioned his favorite football team was Air Force
- Sean asked "Why?"
- Nathan said "Because I went to college there, the Air Force Academy"
- Sean responded: "Nathan, what I just heard you say is thank you, brother. That is who I am, and I'm extremely proud of who I am, my service to this country, and the fact that I went to the Air Force Academy. Nathan, am I hearing you correctly now?"
- Nathan: "Yes."

> "All he said was Air Force... The depth of connection that occurs when you truly listen."

## Step 2: Connecting to Truth, Pain, and Yes Strategy

### Definition
Exploring the truth, uncovering pain points (unmet needs, desires, or challenges), and creating strategies that empower others to say "yes."

> "People do far more to avoid pain than to gain pleasure."

### The Framework: Gap → Acceptability → Solution

**1. The Gap**
- Everyone has a gap between where they are and where they want to be
- The gap may be in: financial abundance, time freedom, impact, relationships, health
- Step 1 uncovers this gap through emotional rapport

**2. Acceptability (or Not)**
- Key question: "How acceptable is it for you to NOT achieve this?"
- If they say it's completely unacceptable, you have established pain
- Without uncovering the pain, nothing happens

**3. The Solution**
- Only after establishing the gap and its unacceptability
- Position your offering as the bridge across the gap

### The Process

1. **Identify the Gap:** Use emotional rapport to uncover what's working and what the challenges are
2. **Lock-In the Gap:** "What I'm hearing you say is... Am I hearing you correctly?"
3. **Establish Acceptability:** "How acceptable would it be to continue living this way?"
4. **Uncover the Pain:** "What will it cost you if this doesn't change?"
5. **Lock-In the Pain:** "What I'm hearing you say is... Am I hearing you correctly?"
6. **Create Yes Strategy:** Position the solution to their specific pain

### Sean's Teaching

> "If we don't get to the gap, acceptability, solution — if we hold the baseball bat upside down and hit it with the handle, forget it."

> "Access road to gap is: past, present, future, plus, minus, open-ended questions, bring nothing back to ourselves."

### Key Insight

The truth may be uncomfortable. People often "delete, dilute, and distort" because they don't want to face their gap. Your role is to:
- Integrously help them see reality
- Not manipulate, but illuminate
- Guide them to their own truth

## Step 3: Creation and Conveyance of Your Heroic Unique Identity (HUI)

### Definition
Sharing your unique story authentically with heroic characteristics. Creating a gravitational pull so they see their future in you.

> "Heroes are ordinary people who choose an uncommon and masterful path."

### The Transition

Before sharing your HUI, ask permission:
> "Would it be okay if I share a few things that may or may not resonate with you?"

This is a crucial transition — you've earned the right to share through Steps 1 and 2.

### Key Elements of HUI

**1. Heroic Characteristics**
- What makes you unique and differentiated
- Your story arc (challenges overcome, growth achieved)
- Authentic sharing of who you are and what you do

**2. Story Arc**
- Share your journey with vulnerability
- Include challenges, setbacks, and how you overcame them
- Connect your story to their situation

**3. Gravitational Pull**
- Create a pull toward you (not pushing toward them)
- Help them see their future in you
- Establish credibility through demonstrated expertise

### Purpose of HUI in the Model

> "It is incredibly important to tell and establish your story with heroic characteristics and a story arc. This creates a gravitational pull toward you and supports the other person in seeing their future in you, which is your outcome by the end of the conversation."

### Multiple HUIs

You don't have just one Heroic Unique Identity — you have different HUIs for different contexts:
- Business/professional
- Personal relationships
- Community/philanthropy
- Family

### Sean's Perspective

> "Step 3 of The 4 Steps is where you introduce who you are and what you are doing in the world, sharing it (only in absolute integrity), and identifying how you can help the person achieve their dreams."

**The Key:** For them to see their future in you.

## Step 4: Agreement Formation

### Definition
The final step — forming agreements built on trust and mutual benefit. This is NOT "closing."

### Agreement Formation vs. Closing

**Critical Distinction:**
> "How would you make you feel (list all the emotions) if you overheard someone saying that they were going to 'close' your mom?"

Closing implies manipulation. Agreement Formation is about:
- Mutual benefit
- Integrous yes
- Trust-based commitment
- Next steps that serve both parties

### The Check-In

Throughout the conversation (especially in Step 4), use:
> "How is this resonating with you?"
> "What are you thinking?"

These check-ins:
- Lock in understanding
- Surface objections early
- Maintain connection
- Prevent disconnect at the end

### Agreement Formation Elements

Per Sean's framework:
- Establish Emotional Rapport ✓
- Be caring, empathetic, precise, respectful, and direct
- Set expectations clearly
- Form clear agreements
- Focus on the desired result (not fear or discomfort)
- Ask open and closed-ended questions, especially "Why?"
- Lean into objections
- Use pattern interrupts and disruptions

### The Integrous Yes

> "The greatest salespeople in the world sell nothing because we just get to integrous, truthful yes."

When Steps 1-3 are done masterfully:
- People sell themselves
- Yes becomes easy
- There is no chasing or desperation
- Integrous scarcity is maintained (preference without attachment)

### Sean's Live Example

After demonstrating with a prospect named "Dash":
> "I didn't tell him to wanna be my partner. He told me all the reasons why he wants to be partnered up. Not desperately. Beautiful integrity, scarcity maintained. So we're not selling, chasing."

### When to Form Agreement

The agreement doesn't have to be a sale:
- Sometimes it's the next meeting
- Sometimes it's a phone call
- Sometimes it's a trial period

Know which is appropriate based on:
- Their readiness
- The complexity of the decision
- The trust established
