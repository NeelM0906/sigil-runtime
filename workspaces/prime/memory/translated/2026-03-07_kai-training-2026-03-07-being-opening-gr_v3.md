**topic** — The Humility Sequence: "The being that goes first with warmth earns the right to go deep with truth. The being that goes first with truth earns nothing but a prospect who feels diagnosed by a stranger."

**context** — This section exposes the most counterintuitive truth in all of Formula application: the practitioner who demonstrates mastery first loses the conversation, while the practitioner who demonstrates humanity first earns the right to deploy mastery later. Command creates resistance. The mirror-versus-monologue distinction operates through Recognition Versus Diagnosis. The Emotional Rapport gap explains the score degradation — Exchange 1 skipped rapport-building entirely, forcing all subsequent exchanges to operate without the relational foundation that makes truth-telling safe.

**SELF (What's Being Navigated):** The practitioner's ego attachment to demonstrating competence early destroys the very competence they're trying to demonstrate. When facing a real prospect, the practitioner's survival instincts default to impressive rather than effective behavior. The humility to be boring, unimpressive, and ordinary in Exchange 1 requires navigating the internal pressure to prove worth immediately.
