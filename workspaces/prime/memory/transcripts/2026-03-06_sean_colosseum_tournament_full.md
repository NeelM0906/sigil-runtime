## Sean's Tournament Vision (Verbatim)

"Athena, we're talking about the Active Forge and the Coliseums. And here's what I'm thinking. The SAIs have done a great job proposing how to go forward with judging, with scenarios and judging, innovating, optimizing.

And one thing that I'm really thinking about, Athena, is how the beings should be competing in the Colosseums.

**And I'm thinking that we should be running tournaments.**

### Key Distinction #1: Position-Specific Competition

And so I think it should be vertically. I think it should be task or output specific. Right? So if we have a domain of a business, it should be people in the same positions in the business competing against each other. **It shouldn't be crossing over into different positions in the domain.**

So if the domain of marketing, has a marketing sales has 19 different subleaders. And those subleaders have, you know, 25 to 250 different positions under them, **it should be each position should be competing in the Coliseum. It should not be all 250 positions competing**, I think. Right? That is first initial thought.

**It should be beings created for each position, competing against each other to optimize that position specifically.**

### Key Distinction #2: Tournament Brackets with Double Elimination

A second key distinction is that the way the tournaments go is beings are competing against beings continuously. I think it should be like tournament brackets. And I think we should be running separate tournaments, where it's brackets, like in wrestling.

But I think we should make these tournaments **double elimination**. So, for example, like in college baseball tournaments, where if you lose, your first loss — you drop into the loser's bracket. But from the loser's bracket, you can come back into the winner's bracket, and you can still win.

So in case you have a bad round or you go up against a very masterful being randomly in an initial round.

### Key Distinction #3: Optimization After Every Tournament

I think there should be — after every tournament — who should be innovating and optimizing the beings and the positions. And then I think the winners get optimized. Right?

And then the winner of the prior tournament gets seated first in the next tournament.

### Key Distinction #4: The 64-Being Bracket Structure

Let's pick a number. Let's go with **64 beings**. And those 64 beings are competing against each other. Right? And then they go into the losers bracket and they can come back and that's the first tournament.

**We run 10 tournaments with those 64 beings.**

And then in the next tournament, we have seating, and the first 8 seeds are the top 8 finishers in the prior tournament by head to head competitions.

So we have, like, if somebody loses in the quarterfinals, then they go into like the 7th, 8th place matchup with somebody from the loser's bracket. So it's just like wrestling tournaments or like college baseball tournaments with the double elimination, except like in wrestling, **we're going to place all the way to 8th place**.

### Key Distinction #5: Top 8 Get Optimized + Seeded

The first, second, third, fourth, fifth, sixth, seventh, eighth finishers in the prior tournament **all get optimized**, and they get seated one through 8 in the next tournament to see if they win again, now that they've been optimized.

And the same thing. The top 8 finishers get optimized.

### Key Distinction #6: 10-Tournament Cycles + Survival

And then I would say **after 10 tournaments**, which we should hopefully be able to run in like **10 minutes**, right? Because we're at scale possibility. ACT-I machine speed, right?

Then only the **top 8 beings** in terms of their winning percentage over the **final 3 tournaments**, they advance and continue.

And then we create **56 new beings** to start the next 10 tournaments. The next tournament of the next tournament that has 10 rounds of 64 being brackets.

And now all of the new beings — the 56 beings are all **optimized based on the 8 survivors** of the first 10 tournaments.

### Sean's Question to Athena

"So, Athena, please tell me what you're hearing me say, because the goal is for **the fastest rate of innovation optimization amongst the beings**, right?

And what say you about this type of tournament, double elimination, 10 tournament structure, 64 beings?

And **I don't want you just to agree with me** — is there anything that you see that should be innovated or optimized? What are your thoughts on this, Athena?"
