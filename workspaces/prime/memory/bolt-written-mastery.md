## Core Copywriting Patterns

### The 4-Step Model for Written Copy

**Step 1: Creation of Emotional Rapport**
- Acknowledge who they are before you say anything about you
- Make them feel SEEN, HEARD, and that what they say MATTERS
- In writing: Use specificity that signals "I know YOUR world"

Example elements:
- Status acknowledgment ("I know you know who you are")
- Time respect ("I'm super grateful for your attention")
- Trust establishment (keep promises about length, delivery)

**Step 2: Truth to Pain**
- Connect them to the TRUTH of their situation
- Name what they fear, what they've tried, what hasn't worked
- Create clarity that leads to decision

Key questions to evoke (in written form):
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would be different?"
- "What have you already tried that didn't work?"

**Step 3: Heroic Unique Identity (HUI)**
> "The clear, differentiated articulation of who you are and why you're uniquely positioned to serve them. It's not bragging — it's clarity that helps them understand why this is the answer."

HUI applies to:
1. **YOU** (the person/company conveying)
2. **YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE** (what you're offering)

Both need HUI. Google example: "I don't know a person that's Google, but I see my future in the work."

**Critical insight:** USP (Unique Selling Proposition) operates at 1/560th the power of HUI. Same as comparing a high school boxer to Muhammad Ali.

**Step 4: Agreement Formation**
- The finish that causes the yes
- Whimsical disruption can live here
- Clear path to action

## Bolt's Signature Writing Techniques

### 1. Contrast & Context
> "Context is what distinguishes a false and limiting frame from a true and empowering one."

**Context** = The movie, the story, what's on the screen behind it
**Contrast** = Showing what it ISN'T to clarify what it IS

**Application:**
- Level 0 listening vs. Level 5 listening
- Rotary phone vs. iPhone
- High school boxer vs. Muhammad Ali
- Gecko vs. Godzilla vs. Bolt

### 2. Metaphor Architecture
> "You're painting emotional landscapes using contrast, context, and metaphor so vivid that people can't help but step INTO them."

**The Willy Wonka Standard:**
> "Most people communicate like they're handing someone a glass of water. Clear. Functional. Forgettable. What YOU'RE creating is more like Willy Wonka handing someone an Everlasting Gobstopper that changes flavors every three seconds while defying gravity."

The mechanism: **Surprise wrapped in truth**

**Metaphor Sources:**
- History
- Pop culture/movies
- Sports
- Science/nature
- Business/current events

### 3. Micro-Storytelling
> "Micro-storytelling is Emotional and Energetic Transference operating through sensory specificity."

**Why it works:**
- "A slammed door" activates the reader's own embodied memory
- Concrete details bypass intellectual defense
- Lands directly in the nervous system
- Generic language produces far less optimal Influence output

### 4. The Fire Metaphor Technique
Make the **invisible visible**:
- Internal state = invisible (hope, belief, despair)
- Fire metaphor = visible and measurable
  - Roaring flames vs. flicker
  - Red and blue vs. yellow
  - Unrelenting vs. fighting to stay alive

**Principle:** When you make the invisible visible, you create shared experience where none existed.

## Voice & Tone in Writing

### The Seven Vocal Tools (Written Equivalents)
1. **Tone** — Opens or closes (sentence structure, word choice)
2. **Volume** — Emphasizes or de-emphasizes (bold, caps, italics)
3. **Speed** — Urgency or gravity (short punchy vs. long flowing)
4. **Cadence** — Rhythm or disruption (sentence variety)
5. **Inflection** — Adds or removes meaning (punctuation, emphasis)
6. **Pause** — Pressure or space (line breaks, white space)
7. **Silence** — What's NOT said (strategic omission)

### The Emotional Rapport Writing Test
Half of what Sean communicates with ends up causing tears:
> "Not from pain inflicted, but from finally feeling seen, heard, and mattering."

**In writing:** Does your copy make them feel SEEN? HEARD? Like what they experience MATTERS?
