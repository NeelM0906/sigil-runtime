## Channel-Specific Strategies

### Email Best Practices

**Timing:**
- **Best send times:** Tuesday-Thursday, 7:00-8:30 AM or 12:00-1:00 PM
- **Avoid:** Monday mornings, Friday afternoons, weekends
- **Follow-up spacing:** 3-7 days between touches

**Format:**
- **Length:** Under 150 words for initial emails
- **Personalization:** Practice name, location, specialty in every email
- **CTA:** Single, clear call-to-action (calendar link)
- **Mobile-first:** Short paragraphs, scannable structure

**Tracking:**
- **Open rates:** Target 25%+ (industry average 18%)
- **Click rates:** Target 5%+ (industry average 2.5%)
- **Reply rates:** Target 3%+ (industry average 1%)
- **Meeting conversion:** Target 15%+ of replies

### Phone Prospecting Best Practices

**Timing:**
- **Best call times:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8:00-9:30 AM or 4:00-5:30 PM
- **Avoid:** Mondays before 10 AM, Fridays after 2 PM
- **Voicemail optimal time:** Under 45 seconds

**Approach:**
- **Gate-keeper relationship:** Polite, professional, brief
- **Decision-maker opening:** Immediate value statement
- **Qualification questions:** Maximum 3 per initial call
- **Next step close:** Always suggest specific meeting time

**Tracking:**
- **Dial-to-connect rate:** Target 15%+ (industry average 8%)
- **Conversation-to-meeting rate:** Target 20%+ 
- **Voicemail return rate:** Target 5%+

## Objection Handling Guide

### "We already have someone handling this."

**Response:**
*"That's great — it means you recognize the problem. Quick question: are you fully satisfied with your current recovery rates and timelines? Most practices we talk to have tried other solutions before coming to us. What made you choose your current provider?"*

**Follow-up probe:**
*"Are they recovering interest on delayed payments too? In [State], that's 2% per month — which adds up fast."*

### "We're too busy to deal with this right now."

**Response:**
*"I completely understand — patient care comes first. That's actually exactly why we handle everything. Your team doesn't lift a finger. We do the audit, file the arbitrations, and recover the money. The only thing you need to do is sign an engagement letter. Can we do 15 minutes this week to see if it's worth pursuing?"*

### "We don't have that many auto accident cases."

**Response:**
*"You might be surprised. Even 10 auto accident cases a month can mean $100K+ in annual PIP billing. And if even 20% of those are delayed or denied, that's $20K+ sitting on the table. Would it be worth a quick look at your numbers?"*

### "We've tried this before and it didn't work."

**Response:**
*"I'm sorry to hear that. Can I ask what approach was tried? [Listen]. The reason most PIP recovery efforts fail is they treat it like regular collections. PIP arbitration is a completely different process — it's legal, not administrative. We have [X]% success rates because we know PIP law inside and out. Would you be open to a second opinion?"*

### "What does this cost us?"

**Response:**
*"Nothing upfront. We work on contingency — we only get paid when you get paid. Our fee is a percentage of what we recover, and it's money you weren't getting anyway. So the ROI is immediate. Can I walk you through how the fee structure works?"*

### "We need to think about it."

**Response:**
*"Absolutely. What specifically would you like to think through? [Listen]. I ask because the one thing I'd want you to consider is that every month you wait, your delayed claims get harder to recover — and the interest that's accruing can't be collected retroactively forever. What would help you feel confident about moving forward?"*

### "Send me some information."

**Response:**
*"Happy to. To make sure I send you the most relevant information, can I ask two quick questions? What's your biggest PIP frustration right now, and roughly how many auto accident patients do you see monthly? That way I can include case studies from practices similar to yours."*
