# LEAD-QUALIFICATION.md - PIP Lead Qualification Framework

## Qualification Philosophy

**Not every practice with PIP issues is a good client.** 

The goal is to identify emergency medicine and orthopedic practices that:
1. **Have sufficient PIP volume** to justify our investment
2. **Experience real payment pain** that we can solve
3. **Possess decision-making authority** to engage our services
4. **Demonstrate financial capacity** to invest in recovery
5. **Show commitment to action** beyond casual interest

## Pre-Call Qualification Checklist

### Practice Profile Requirements

**Size & Scope (Must Meet 3 of 4):**
- [ ] **Physician count:** 3+ physicians (emergency medicine) or 2+ (orthopedic)
- [ ] **Patient volume:** 10,000+ annual visits (ER) or 1,500+ (orthopedic)
- [ ] **Geographic coverage:** NJ or NY location in target counties
- [ ] **Auto accident focus:** Website mentions MVA, trauma, auto injuries

**Financial Indicators (Must Meet 2 of 3):**
- [ ] **Revenue scale:** $1M+ estimated annual practice revenue
- [ ] **Technology investment:** Modern EMR, practice management systems
- [ ] **Growth signals:** Recent expansions, new physician recruitment

**Accessibility (Must Meet 1 of 2):**
- [ ] **Decision-maker identified:** Practice owner, administrator, or revenue manager
- [ ] **Contact information verified:** Direct phone, email, or LinkedIn connection

### Red Flags (Automatic Disqualification)

**Practice Structure:**
- [ ] **Solo practitioner only** (unless high-volume trauma specialist)
- [ ] **Concierge/cash-only practice** (minimal insurance billing)
- [ ] **Academic/research-focused** (low clinical volume)
- [ ] **Retiring/closing practice** (within 12 months)

**Financial Constraints:**
- [ ] **Current bankruptcy/financial distress** (public records)
- [ ] **Recent malpractice settlements** affecting practice operations
- [ ] **Existing exclusive collection contracts** (locked in for 12+ months)

**Competitive Conflicts:**
- [ ] **Callagy Law existing client** (separate matter conflicts)
- [ ] **Active litigation against us** (opponent in current case)
- [ ] **Competitor exclusive representation** (direct conflict firms)

## Discovery Call Framework

### Pre-Call Preparation (5 minutes)

**Research Review:**
- Practice website and physician backgrounds
- Recent news, expansions, or leadership changes
- LinkedIn profiles of meeting attendees
- Local market competition and auto accident trends

**Agenda Preparation:**
- Meeting objective: Qualify PIP pain and recovery potential
- Key questions prioritized by importance
- Value proposition summary for this specific practice
- Next step options (proposal, audit, follow-up meeting)

### Opening (5 minutes)

**Rapport Building:**
*"Thank you for taking time from patient care to discuss your PIP challenges. I know how busy emergency medicine/orthopedic practices are, especially with auto accident cases."*

**Agenda Setting:**
*"I'd like to understand your current PIP payment situation, share how other [emergency medicine groups/orthopedic practices] have solved similar challenges, and determine if there's a fit for us to help you recover what's owed. Does that sound reasonable?"*

**Permission to Ask Questions:**
*"To give you the most relevant information, I'll need to ask some specific questions about your PIP volumes and payment experiences. Is that okay?"*

### PIP Volume Assessment (10 minutes)

**Annual Case Volume:**
- *"How many auto accident patients do you treat annually?"*
- *"What percentage of your total patient volume is auto-related?"*
- *"Do you track PIP cases separately from other insurance billing?"*

**Revenue Scale:**
- *"What's your approximate annual PIP billing amount?"*
- *"How does that compare to your total practice revenue?"*
- *"Are auto accident cases more profitable than other cases?"*

**Payer Mix:**
- *"Which auto insurers do you work with most frequently?"*
- *"Do different insurers pay at different rates or speeds?"*
- *"Any insurers you've stopped working with due to payment issues?"*

**Qualification Thresholds:**
- **Minimum viable:** $100K+ annual PIP billing
- **Strong candidate:** $250K+ annual PIP billing
- **Ideal client:** $500K+ annual PIP billing

### Pain Point Discovery (10 minutes)

**Payment Timeline Issues:**
- *"What's your typical PIP payment timeline from claim to payment?"*
- *"How often do claims exceed the 30-day (NY) or 60-day (NJ) legal requirement?"*
- *"What's the longest you've waited for a PIP payment?"*

**Denial & Dispute Patterns:**
- *"What percentage of your PIP claims get denied on first submission?"*
- *"What are the most common denial reasons you see?"*
- *"Do you appeal denials, and what's your success rate?"*

**Outstanding Receivables:**
- *"How much PIP money do you have outstanding right now?"*
- *"How old are your oldest unpaid PIP claims?"*
- *"Have you written off any PIP claims as uncollectable?"*

**Administrative Burden:**
- *"Who handles PIP billing and collections in your practice?"*
- *"How much staff time goes to PIP follow-up each week?"*
- *"Do you use any special systems or vendors for PIP claims?"*

**Pain Severity Scoring:**
- **Critical (20 points):** $50K+ outstanding, 6+ month delays, 40%+ denial rates
- **Significant (15 points):** $25K+ outstanding, 3+ month delays, 25%+ denial rates
- **Moderate (10 points):** $10K+ outstanding, 60+ day delays, 15%+ denial rates
- **Minor (5 points):** <$10K outstanding, occasional delays, <10% denial rates

### Solution Interest Assessment (5 minutes)

**Current Strategies:**
- *"What have you tried to improve your PIP collections?"*
- *"Do you have legal representation for PIP disputes?"*
- *"Have you considered arbitration for delayed claims?"*

**Investment Readiness:**
- *"If you could recover more PIP money faster, what would that mean for your practice?"*
- *"What would you expect to invest to improve your PIP recovery rate?"*
- *"Who else would need to be involved in a decision to hire a recovery firm?"*

**Action Orientation:**
- *"How urgent is solving your PIP collection challenges?"*
- *"What would need to happen for you to move forward with a solution?"*
- *"What's holding you back from addressing this aggressively right now?"*

### Authority & Process Discovery (5 minutes)

**Decision-Making Process:**
- *"Who makes decisions about legal or collection services for the practice?"*
- *"Is there a formal vendor approval process we'd need to follow?"*
- *"What's your typical timeline for evaluating and selecting new services?"*

**Budget & Investment:**
- *"Do you have a budget allocated for legal or collection services?"*
- *"How do you typically structure fee arrangements for these services?"*
- *"What ROI would justify investing in PIP recovery services?"*

**Implementation Considerations:**
- *"What would your ideal recovery service look like?"*
- *"Any concerns about working with a collection or legal firm?"*
- *"How important is it that we understand your specific practice type?"*

### Total Qualification Score

| Score Range | Classification | Action |
|-------------|----------------|--------|
| **80-100** | **Hot Qualified Lead** | Immediate proposal preparation, expedited follow-up |
| **60-79** | **Warm Qualified Lead** | Standard qualification process, proposal within 1 week |
| **40-59** | **Cool Lead** | Extended nurture sequence, quarterly check-ins |
| **20-39** | **Poor Fit** | Polite decline, potential future re-qualification |
| **0-19** | **Disqualified** | End engagement, document reasons |

## Handoff Package Requirements

### For Hot Qualified Leads (80+ points)

**Practice Profile:**
- Complete practice demographics and contact information
- Physician count, specialties, and years in practice
- Annual patient volume and revenue estimates
- Location details and target market characteristics

**PIP Assessment:**
- Annual PIP billing volume and payer mix
- Outstanding receivables amount and aging
- Current denial rates and common denial reasons
- Payment timeline patterns and worst-case examples

**Decision-Making Intel:**
- Primary decision-maker name, title, and background
- Secondary influencers and stakeholders
- Budget authority level and approval process
- Previous experience with legal/collection services

**Urgency Factors:**
- Immediate pain points driving urgency
- Timeline for decision-making
- Competitive considerations or alternatives being evaluated
- Success criteria and ROI expectations

### For Warm Qualified Leads (60-79 points)

**Core Information:**
- Basic practice profile and contact details
- PIP volume estimates and pain point summary
- Decision-maker identification and authority level
- Preliminary interest level and concerns

**Next Steps:**
- Recommended follow-up timeline and approach
- Additional information needed for full qualification
- Potential objections or obstacles to address
- Strategic considerations for conversion

### Disqualification Documentation

**For Poor Fit/Disqualified Prospects:**
- Specific reasons for disqualification
- Potential future re-qualification criteria
- Referral opportunities to other services
- Market intelligence gathered for future targeting
