## NEW JERSEY PIP LAW

### Governing Statute
**N.J.S.A. 39:6A-4 through 39:6A-5** — New Jersey Automobile Reparation Reform Act

### Coverage Requirements
- **Mandatory PIP coverage** for all NJ auto insurance policies
- **Minimum coverage:** $15,000 per person per accident
- **Standard coverage:** $250,000 (most common)
- **Coverage applies to:** Medical expenses, income continuation, essential services, funeral expenses

### Provider Obligations

**Notification Requirements:**
- **21-day rule:** Provider must notify insurer within 21 days of beginning treatment
- **Failure consequences:** Claim may be denied, BUT court may allow refiling within 14 days if no substantial prejudice to insurer
- **Commissioner standards:** Consider length of delay and patterns of noncompliance

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Complete treatment records and medical reports
- Itemized billing with CPT/ICD codes
- Medical necessity documentation
- Patient authorization and assignment of benefits

### Insurer Obligations

**Investigation Timeline:**
- **10 working days** from notification to begin investigation
- Must request all needed documentation within this window

**Payment Timeline:**
- **60 days** from receipt of claim and supporting documentation
- **45-day extension** allowed IF insurer provides written justification
- **Partial claims:** Each component overdue if unpaid within 60 days of supporting notice

**Penalties for Delay:**
- Interest on overdue amounts (per N.J.S.A. 39:6A-5)
- Potential bad faith claims for unreasonable delays
- Attorney fees and costs in successful arbitration/litigation

### Common Insurer Tactics in NJ

| Tactic | Description | Counter-Strategy |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| **Unnecessary documentation requests** | Repeated requests for already-provided records | Certified mail with tracking; detailed response logs |
| **IME (Independent Medical Exam) abuse** | Ordering exams to suspend benefits | Challenge IME findings; request examiner credentials |
| **Medical necessity denials** | Blanket denials of treatment appropriateness | Peer-to-peer review requests; detailed clinical rationale |
| **Fee schedule disputes** | Reducing payments based on disputed fee schedules | Arbitration on fee adequacy; UCR documentation |
| **Investigation extensions** | Using 45-day extension without valid justification | Challenge written justification; demand compliance |

### NJ PIP Arbitration Process

**Filing:**
- File through designated PIP arbitration organization
- Must include all supporting documentation
- Filing fee varies by claim amount

**Timeline:**
- Hearing typically scheduled within 3-6 months
- Decision rendered within 30 days of hearing
- Interest accrues from date payment was due

**Outcomes:**
- Arbitrator may award full claim amount + interest
- Attorney fees recoverable in successful cases
- Appeal rights limited (binding arbitration in most cases)

## NEW YORK PIP LAW (NO-FAULT)

### Governing Statute
**NY Insurance Law § 5101-5109** — Comprehensive Motor Vehicle Insurance Reparations Act
**11 NYCRR 65 (Regulation 68)** — No-Fault Insurance Regulations

### Coverage Requirements
- **Mandatory no-fault coverage** for all NY auto insurance policies
- **Minimum coverage:** $50,000 per person per accident (Basic Economic Loss)
- **Coverage applies to:** Medical expenses, lost wages (80% up to $2,000/month), other reasonable expenses
- **No monetary threshold** for medical expense claims

### Provider Obligations

**Assignment of Benefits (AOB):**
- Provider may accept direct assignment from patient
- Provides standing to pursue arbitration directly against insurer
- Form NF-AOB required

**Billing Requirements:**
- **NF-3 form** (verification of treatment) required
- Submit within 45 days of treatment
- Follow NY Workers' Compensation fee schedule for billing amounts
- Include all supporting documentation with initial submission

**Verification Response:**
- Must respond to insurer verification requests within 30 days
- Failure to respond may delay or forfeit claim

### Insurer Obligations

**Verification Timeline:**
- **10 business days** from receipt of claim to request verification (additional documentation)
- **30 calendar days** from receipt of verification to request additional verification
- All verification must be completed within 30 days of receipt of prescribed forms

**Payment Timeline:**
- **30 calendar days** from receipt of proof of claim (or completion of verification)
- Clock starts when insurer has all necessary documentation
- **Denial deadline:** Must deny within 30 days or claim is deemed accepted (overdue)

**Penalties for Delay:**
- **2% per month interest** on overdue payments (24% annually!)
- Attorney fees in successful arbitration
- Potential regulatory action for pattern of delayed payments

### Key NY Advantage: The 2% Monthly Interest

**This is our most powerful selling point in NY.**

| Months Delayed | Interest Accrued | On $10,000 Claim | On $50,000 Claim |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 3 months | 6% | $600 | $3,000 |
| 6 months | 12% | $1,200 | $6,000 |
| 10 months | 20% | $2,000 | $10,000 |
| 12 months | 24% | $2,400 | $12,000 |

**Translation for prospects:** *"Every month they delay paying you, they owe you 2% more. After 10 months, a $50,000 claim becomes a $60,000 claim. We collect ALL of it."*

### Common Insurer Tactics in NY

| Tactic | Description | Counter-Strategy |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| **Peer review denials** | Hiring peer reviewers to deny medical necessity | Challenge reviewer qualifications; demand peer-to-peer |
| **IME cutoffs** | Using IME to terminate benefits mid-treatment | Contest IME methodology; provide contradicting clinical evidence |
| **Verification abuse** | Endless verification requests to delay clock | Track verification timeline; file when deadline expires |
| **Fee schedule disputes** | Paying below WC fee schedule rates | Arbitrate for full fee schedule amount |
| **Late denial** | Denying after 30-day window (technically overdue) | File arbitration — late denial = overdue claim with interest |

### NY PIP Arbitration Process

**Filing (through AAA — American Arbitration Association):**
- Provider must have valid Assignment of Benefits (AOB)
- File AR form (Applicant's Request for Arbitration)
- Filing fee: $40 per claim
- Include all NF forms, medical records, denial letters

**Current Timeline:**
- **10-month average wait** for hearing (as of 2025-2026)
- AAA hiring 100+ new arbitrators to reduce backlog
- Decision typically within 30 days of hearing
- Interest accrues entire time (2%/month = ~20% by hearing)

**Settlement Strategy:**
- Many insurers settle pre-hearing to avoid interest accumulation
- Aggressive settlement pursuit can yield payment in **60 days or less**
- Settlement often preferred by both parties vs. 10-month wait

**Outcomes:**
- Arbitrator may award full claim + interest + attorney fees
- Master arbitration (appeal) available within 30 days
- Court review limited to fraud, bias, or exceeding arbitrator authority

## CROSS-STATE COMPARISON

| Factor | New Jersey | New York |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Minimum PIP Coverage** | $15,000 | $50,000 |
| **Payment Deadline** | 60 days (+45 extension) | 30 days |
| **Interest on Delays** | Varies | 2% per month |
| **Notification Requirement** | 21 days from treatment start | 45 days from treatment |
| **Arbitration Body** | State-designated | AAA |
| **Arbitration Timeline** | 3-6 months typical | 10 months typical |
| **Fee Schedule** | NJ-specific | NY Workers' Comp schedule |
| **AOB (Assignment of Benefits)** | Available | Available (NF-AOB) |
