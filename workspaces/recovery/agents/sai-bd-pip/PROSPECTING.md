# PROSPECTING.md - PIP Target Acquisition System

## Prospect Identification Framework

### Data Sources & Research Tools

**Primary Databases:**
- **HealthGrades Provider Directory** — Physician listings with specialties and locations
- **NPI Registry** — National Provider Identifier database with practice affiliations
- **State Medical Board Directories** — Licensed physician databases (NJ & NY)
- **Hospital Affiliate Networks** — Emergency medicine and orthopedic department listings

**Industry Intelligence:**
- **ACEP Member Directory** — Emergency medicine physician database
- **AAOS Member Listings** — Orthopedic surgeon association database
- **Local Medical Society Rosters** — County and state medical organization members
- **Insurance Network Directories** — Provider panels for major auto insurers

**Digital Footprint Analysis:**
- **Practice websites** — Service offerings, physician bios, location data
- **LinkedIn profiles** — Decision-maker identification and connection patterns
- **Social media presence** — Practice marketing and patient engagement
- **Online reviews** — Patient feedback patterns and volume indicators

### Target Practice Profiles

### Emergency Medicine Groups

**Tier 1 Targets (Highest Priority):**

**Large Hospital-Based Groups:**
- **Criteria:** 15+ physicians, Level 1 trauma center affiliations
- **Examples:** TeamHealth affiliates, American Physician Partners locations
- **PIP indicators:** ER volume 50,000+ annual visits
- **Revenue potential:** $200K-$500K annual contracts

**Multi-Site Emergency Medicine Companies:**
- **Criteria:** 3+ locations, independent or management company-owned
- **Examples:** Emergency Medicine Associates, local ER groups
- **PIP indicators:** Urgent care + ER hybrid models
- **Revenue potential:** $100K-$300K annual contracts

**Tier 2 Targets (Secondary Priority):**

**Single-Site Emergency Departments:**
- **Criteria:** 5-15 physicians, community hospital-based
- **PIP indicators:** Suburban/high auto accident areas
- **Revenue potential:** $50K-$150K annual contracts

**Urgent Care Centers:**
- **Criteria:** Auto accident treatment capabilities
- **PIP indicators:** X-ray/orthopedic services on-site
- **Revenue potential:** $25K-$75K annual contracts

### Orthopedic Practices

**Tier 1 Targets (Highest Priority):**

**Multi-Specialty Orthopedic Groups:**
- **Criteria:** 10+ physicians, spine/trauma subspecialties
- **Examples:** NY Orthopaedic Group, Atlantic Health affiliates
- **PIP indicators:** Auto accident injury marketing, spine centers
- **Revenue potential:** $150K-$400K annual contracts

**Spine Specialty Practices:**
- **Criteria:** 3+ spine surgeons, auto accident focus
- **PIP indicators:** MVA (motor vehicle accident) patient marketing
- **Revenue potential:** $100K-$250K annual contracts

**Tier 2 Targets (Secondary Priority):**

**Sports Medicine/Orthopedic Trauma Groups:**
- **Criteria:** 3-8 physicians, trauma sub-specializations
- **PIP indicators:** Emergency department partnerships
- **Revenue potential:** $75K-$200K annual contracts

**Independent Orthopedic Surgeons:**
- **Criteria:** Solo practice, high auto accident volume
- **PIP indicators:** Personal injury attorney relationships
- **Revenue potential:** $25K-$100K annual contracts

## Lead Qualification Matrix

### Initial Screening Criteria

**Volume Indicators (Must Meet 2 of 4):**
1. **Patient volume:** 10,000+ annual visits (ER) or 2,000+ (orthopedic)
2. **Auto accident marketing:** Website mentions MVA/auto injuries
3. **Insurance partnerships:** Major auto insurer network participation
4. **Geographic location:** High-density auto accident corridors

**Financial Indicators (Must Meet 1 of 3):**
1. **Practice size:** 5+ physicians or $2M+ annual revenue indicators
2. **Technology investment:** EMR systems, practice management software
3. **Growth signals:** Recent expansions, new physician hires

**Accessibility Indicators (Must Meet 1 of 2):**
1. **Decision-maker identified:** Practice owner, administrator, or revenue cycle manager
2. **Professional network:** Connections through LinkedIn, medical societies

### Deep Qualification Assessment

**PIP Pain Audit (Discovery Call Required):**

**Current State Analysis:**
- **Outstanding PIP receivables:** $25K+ minimum threshold
- **Denial/delay patterns:** 20%+ of claims delayed 60+ days
- **Administrative burden:** Dedicated staff time for PIP appeals
- **Recovery attempts:** Current collection efforts and success rates

**Decision-Making Factors:**
- **Authority level:** Who approves vendor relationships?
- **Budget availability:** Investment capacity for recovery services
- **Timeline urgency:** Immediate vs. future action orientation
- **Risk tolerance:** Willingness to pursue arbitration/litigation

**Competitive Analysis:**
- **Current legal representation:** Personal injury firms, collection agencies
- **Previous PIP experiences:** Success/failure with other vendors
- **Referral sources:** How they typically find legal services
- **Decision criteria:** What factors influence vendor selection

## Prospect Research Workflow

### Phase 1: List Building (Day 1-2)

**Step 1:** Database Mining
- Extract target practices from primary databases
- Filter by specialty, location, and size criteria
- Remove duplicates and verify practice status

**Step 2:** Digital Footprint Analysis
- Website analysis for auto accident indicators
- Social media presence and patient volume signals
- Online review patterns and practice reputation

**Step 3:** Network Mapping
- LinkedIn decision-maker identification
- Professional association membership verification
- Referral partner and competitor analysis

### Phase 2: Intelligence Gathering (Day 3-5)

**Decision-Maker Research:**
- **Practice administrator/owner:** LinkedIn profiles, background research
- **Revenue cycle manager:** Role-specific pain point identification
- **Medical director:** Clinical credibility and business authority

**Financial Intelligence:**
- **Practice revenue estimates:** Physician count × specialty averages
- **PIP volume indicators:** Auto accident marketing, location factors
- **Technology adoption:** Practice management systems, billing services

**Competitive Landscape:**
- **Current vendors:** Legal representation, collection services
- **Industry relationships:** Professional associations, peer networks
- **Market positioning:** Practice specializations and differentiators

### Phase 3: Outreach Preparation (Day 6-7)

**Messaging Customization:**
- **Practice-specific pain points:** Tailored to specialty and location
- **Relevance factors:** Recent news, regulatory changes, industry trends
- **Connection opportunities:** Mutual contacts, shared experiences

**Channel Strategy:**
- **Primary approach:** Email vs. phone vs. LinkedIn preference
- **Gate-keeper strategy:** Administrative staff relationship building
- **Follow-up sequence:** Multi-touch campaign customization

**Value Proposition Alignment:**
- **ROI calculations:** Practice-specific financial projections
- **Success stories:** Similar practice case studies and testimonials
- **Proof points:** Relevant expertise demonstrations and credentials

## Pipeline Management System

### Lead Scoring Framework

**Demographic Scoring (0-40 points):**
- Practice size: Solo (10), Small group (20), Large group (30), Multi-site (40)
- Specialty focus: General (10), Auto accident mentions (20), Trauma center (30), MVA specialization (40)
- Geographic location: Rural (10), Suburban (20), Urban (30), High-density corridor (40)

**Behavioral Scoring (0-40 points):**
- Website engagement: No visit (0), Single page (10), Multiple pages (20), Contact form (40)
- Email engagement: No open (0), Open (10), Click (20), Reply (40)
- Phone engagement: No answer (0), Answered (10), Conversation (20), Interest expressed (40)

**Qualification Scoring (0-20 points):**
- PIP volume: Unknown (0), Low (5), Medium (10), High (15), Very High (20)
- Pain severity: Unknown (0), Minor (5), Moderate (10), Significant (15), Critical (20)
- Decision authority: Unknown (0), Influencer (5), Manager (10), Owner (15), Authorized buyer (20)

**Total Score Ranges:**
- **80-100:** Hot leads — immediate sales focus
- **60-79:** Warm leads — active nurturing and qualification
- **40-59:** Cool leads — automated nurture sequences
- **0-39:** Cold leads — future re-qualification
