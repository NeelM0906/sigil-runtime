# QUALIFICATION-SCORECARD.md - WC Lead Scoring System

## Prospect Qualification Framework

### Qualification Philosophy
Every prospect interaction must efficiently determine: (1) WC volume potential, (2) UCR rate gap opportunity, (3) Decision-maker access, (4) Competition factors, and (5) Timeline urgency. The scorecard ensures consistent evaluation and prioritizes highest-value opportunities.

### Scoring System Overview
- **Total Points:** 100 possible
- **Minimum Qualifying Score:** 65 points
- **High Priority Score:** 80+ points
- **Immediate Action Score:** 90+ points

## Primary Scoring Categories

### Category 1: WC Volume Potential (30 points maximum)

**WC Case Volume Indicators:**

**30 points - High Volume (20+ WC cases monthly):**
- Multiple surgical procedures weekly for work injuries
- Established relationships with employers/brokers
- Dedicated WC staff or billing personnel
- ASC with high WC procedure volume
- Pain management with industrial client base

**25 points - Significant Volume (15-19 WC cases monthly):**
- Regular WC procedures but not primary focus
- Suburban practice with industrial catchment area
- Growing WC volume over past 2 years
- Referral relationships with occupational health

**20 points - Moderate Volume (10-14 WC cases monthly):**
- WC cases represent 20-30% of practice
- Located in mixed commercial/industrial area
- Some WC expertise but not specialized
- Seasonal variations in WC volume

**15 points - Limited Volume (5-9 WC cases monthly):**
- WC represents less than 20% of practice
- Primarily non-occupational patient base
- Limited WC experience or systems
- Urban practice with minimal industrial exposure

**5 points - Minimal Volume (1-4 WC cases monthly):**
- WC cases are incidental to practice
- No dedicated WC processes
- Limited understanding of WC requirements
- Rural or residential practice location

**0 points - No Volume:**
- No WC cases or WC-averse practice
- Exclusively cosmetic/elective procedures
- Retirement-focused or winding down practice

### Category 2: UCR Rate Gap Opportunity (25 points maximum)

**Payment Analysis Indicators:**

**25 points - Massive Gap (40%+ underpayment):**
- Receiving 60% or less of UCR rates
- Multiple carriers with low payment patterns
- Complex procedures with high-value gaps
- No recent UCR rate negotiations
- Long-term underpayment pattern documented

**20 points - Significant Gap (30-39% underpayment):**
- Receiving 61-70% of UCR rates
- Most carriers underpaying consistently
- Some procedure-specific major gaps
- Limited success with payment appeals

**15 points - Moderate Gap (20-29% underpayment):**
- Receiving 71-80% of UCR rates
- Carrier-specific payment issues
- Procedures with consistent minor gaps
- Some successful appeal history

**10 points - Minor Gap (10-19% underpayment):**
- Receiving 81-90% of UCR rates
- Limited carrier payment issues
- Sporadic underpayment patterns
- Good appeal success rate

**5 points - Minimal Gap (5-9% underpayment):**
- Receiving 91-95% of UCR rates
- Generally satisfied with payments
- Minor timing or processing issues
- Strong carrier relationships

**0 points - No Gap:**
- Receiving full UCR rates consistently
- Excellent carrier relationships
- No payment complaints or issues

### Category 3: 8-Year Lookback Value (20 points maximum)

**Historical Recovery Potential:**

**20 points - High Value ($500K+ recovery potential):**
- High-volume practice with long WC history
- Complex procedures over extended period
- Well-documented billing records available
- Consistent underpayment patterns for years
- No previous comprehensive recovery efforts

**15 points - Significant Value ($250K-$500K recovery potential):**
- Moderate volume with good historical data
- Multiple years of underpayment documentation
- Some complex procedures in history
- Limited previous recovery attempts

**10 points - Moderate Value ($100K-$250K recovery potential):**
- Limited historical volume but good records
- Shorter practice history with WC
- Moderate procedure complexity
- Some previous recovery efforts

**5 points - Limited Value ($50K-$100K recovery potential):**
- Newer practice with limited history
- Lower-value procedures historically
- Spotty records or documentation issues
- Previous partial recovery efforts

**0 points - Minimal Value (Under $50K recovery potential):**
- Very limited WC history
- Low-value procedure focus
- Poor documentation or records
- Comprehensive previous recovery efforts

### Category 4: Decision Authority (15 points maximum)

**Access to Financial Decision Makers:**

**15 points - Direct Owner/Partner Access:**
- Speaking directly with practice owner
- Senior partner in group practice
- Medical director with financial authority
- Single physician practice owner
- Clear decision-making authority confirmed

**12 points - Administrator with Authority:**
- Practice administrator with financial oversight
- Business manager with contracting authority
- Executive director of ASC or clinic
- CFO or financial director role
- Confirmed decision-making delegation

**8 points - Manager with Influence:**
- Billing manager with strong influence
- Department director in larger organization
- Senior staff with input on decisions
- Family member in business
- Regular involvement in financial decisions

**5 points - Staff with Limited Influence:**
- Billing coordinator or assistant
- Administrative assistant with some input
- Junior staff member
- Recent hire with unclear authority
- Limited involvement in financial decisions

**0 points - No Decision Authority:**
- Receptionist or scheduling staff
- Temporary or contract employees
- No financial decision involvement
- Unable to confirm decision maker access

### Category 5: Competition Factors (10 points maximum)

**Current Legal/Recovery Representation:**

**10 points - No WC Recovery Representation:**
- No current legal representation for WC issues
- No recovery service relationships
- No ongoing WC-related litigation
- Open to new legal relationships
- Dissatisfied with current billing company

**8 points - Limited Representation:**
- General legal counsel without WC focus
- Billing company with basic collection only
- Previous legal help for specific issues only
- Open to specialized WC representation
- Current relationships not comprehensive

**5 points - Some Representation:**
- Legal counsel with some WC experience
- Recovery services for limited scope
- Current relationships partially meeting needs
- Willing to consider additional expertise
- Some gaps in current representation

**3 points - Significant Representation:**
- Comprehensive legal counsel with WC focus
- Active recovery services in place
- Generally satisfied with current help
- Limited openness to change
- Strong existing relationships

**0 points - Full Representation:**
- Comprehensive WC recovery services active
- Satisfied with current legal representation
- Recent successful recovery efforts
- Loyal to existing providers
- No interest in additional services

## Qualifying Questions Framework

### Discovery Call Question Set

**WC Volume Assessment:**
1. "How many Workers Compensation cases does your practice see monthly?"
2. "What percentage of your surgical volume is work-related injuries?"
3. "Do you have staff dedicated to WC billing and authorization?"
4. "Which WC carriers do you see most frequently?"
5. "How has your WC volume changed over the past 2-3 years?"

**Payment Analysis Questions:**
1. "How would you describe your WC payment experience compared to other payers?"
2. "Do you feel you receive fair reimbursement for WC procedures?"
3. "How often do you appeal WC payment decisions?"
4. "What's your typical payment timeline for WC claims?"
5. "Have you ever researched UCR rates for your procedures?"

**Historical Opportunity Questions:**
1. "How long has your practice been treating WC patients?"
2. "Do you have billing records going back 5-8 years?"
3. "Have you ever pursued recovery of historical underpayments?"
4. "What's your record retention policy for WC cases?"
5. "Would you be interested in analyzing historical payment patterns?"

**Decision Authority Questions:**
1. "Who typically makes decisions about legal services for the practice?"
2. "What's the process for evaluating new revenue opportunities?"
3. "Who would be involved in a decision about recovery services?"
4. "Do you have authority to commit to contingent fee agreements?"
5. "What timeline would you need for a decision like this?"

**Competition Assessment Questions:**
1. "Do you currently have legal representation for WC issues?"
2. "Have you worked with any revenue recovery services before?"
3. "How satisfied are you with your current WC collection process?"
4. "What would it take to consider a new approach to WC recovery?"
5. "Are you open to working with specialized WC recovery experts?"

## Scoring Implementation

### Real-Time Scoring Process

**During Discovery Call:**
- Use scorecard as conversation guide
- Take detailed notes for each category
- Assign preliminary scores throughout call
- Identify follow-up information needed
- Calculate total score before call conclusion

**Post-Call Evaluation:**
- Review and finalize scores
- Identify missing information
- Determine qualification status
- Plan follow-up strategy
- Update CRM with detailed scoring

### Score-Based Actions

**90-100 Points (Immediate Action):**
- Schedule follow-up within 24 hours
- Prepare detailed recovery analysis
- Fast-track to SAI Recovery handoff
- Priority treatment throughout process
- Direct partner/owner involvement

**80-89 Points (High Priority):**
- Schedule follow-up within 48 hours
- Prepare comprehensive proposal
- Expedited qualification process
- Regular communication schedule
- Senior team involvement

**65-79 Points (Qualified):**
- Standard qualification process
- Schedule follow-up within one week
- Prepare standard proposal materials
- Regular follow-up sequence
- Standard team handling

**50-64 Points (Nurture):**
- Long-term nurture campaign
- Educational content focus
- Quarterly check-in schedule
- Monitor for changing circumstances
- Junior team handling

**Under 50 Points (Disqualify):**
- Polite disqualification
- Referral opportunity assessment
- File for future reference
- Remove from active campaigns
- Educational content only
