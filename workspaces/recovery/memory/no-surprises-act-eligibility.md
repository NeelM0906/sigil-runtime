# No Surprises Act — IDR Eligibility Reference Guide
_Built: 2026-03-01 | Owner: SAI Recovery_

## CRITICAL FIRST POINT — What NSA Does NOT Cover

**Workers' comp, PIP, and auto insurance are EXCLUDED from NSA/IDR.**

The No Surprises Act only applies to **health insurance** disputes. Callagy Recovery's core PIP and workers' comp work uses different arbitration systems entirely (Forthright for NJ PIP, state WC boards for workers' comp). NSA is a separate track for commercial health insurance out-of-network disputes.

---

## What the NSA/IDR Process Covers

Three qualifying service categories — must be ALL of these:
1. **Out-of-network** provider or facility
2. **Patient has health insurance** (commercial, employer-sponsored, marketplace)
3. Service falls into one of:
   - Emergency services at any facility (OON provider/facility)
   - Non-emergency services by OON provider at an **in-network facility** (hospital, freestanding ER, ASC, certain urgent care)
   - Air ambulance by OON provider

## Claims NOT Eligible for NSA/IDR — How to Tell

### Category 1: Wrong Insurance Type
| Insurance Type | Eligible? | Why |
|---------------|-----------|-----|
| Commercial health insurance (ACA, employer) | ✅ Yes | NSA applies |
| Medicare | ❌ No | Federal program, separate rules |
| Medicaid | ❌ No | State program, separate rules |
| Workers' Compensation | ❌ No | State WC system governs |
| Auto PIP | ❌ No | State PIP/no-fault system governs |
| TRICARE/VA | ❌ No | Federal military programs |
| Self-pay/uninsured | ❌ No | No insurance plan to dispute |

### Category 2: Wrong Service Type
- In-network provider (no surprise bill)
- Non-emergency services at an out-of-network facility (patient chose OON facility)
- Services at non-qualifying sites: medical offices, birthing centers, skilled nursing, home health
- Elective care where patient signed NSA consent/waiver to receive OON care
- Services not covered by the plan at all

### Category 3: State Law Supersedes
- If the state has its own surprise billing law that applies → federal IDR excluded
- States with their own laws: NY, CA, TX, IL, and others
- Must check state by state for each claim

### Category 4: Procedural Failures
- **Didn't complete 30-business-day open negotiation first** (most common error)
- Filed IDR initiation more than 4 business days after negotiation period ended
- Improper batching (batched claims must share: same provider, same plan, same service type, same 30/90-day window)
- Already resolved via negotiation
- Already paid in full

### Category 5: Already Under Negotiation or Contract
- Claim is subject to an existing contract rate
- Dispute already resolved or pending state arbitration

## How to Determine Eligibility — Step by Step Checklist

**Step 1: Insurance type check**
☐ Is this a commercial health insurance claim? (NOT workers' comp, NOT PIP, NOT Medicare/Medicaid)
→ If NO → stop, use the appropriate system (Forthright for PIP, WC board for workers' comp)

**Step 2: Provider network status**
☐ Was the provider out-of-network with the patient's plan at time of service?
→ If NO (provider was in-network) → not eligible

**Step 3: Service type check**
☐ Was it emergency services? OR
☐ Non-emergency at an in-network facility? OR
☐ Air ambulance?
→ If NONE of these → not eligible

**Step 4: Consent waiver check**
☐ Did patient sign a valid NSA consent/waiver to receive OON care?
→ If YES and properly executed → not eligible (patient chose OON)

**Step 5: State law check**
☐ Does the state have a surprise billing law that applies to this claim?
→ If YES → may need to use state process instead

**Step 6: Open negotiation status**
☐ Has the 30-business-day open negotiation period been completed?
→ If NO → cannot file yet
☐ Was IDR initiated within 4 business days of negotiation ending?
→ If NO → may be time-barred

**Step 7: Batching rules (if batching claims)**
☐ Same initiating party?
☐ Same respondent?
☐ Same service type?
☐ Same 30 or 90-day period?
→ If any mismatch → must file separately

## Key Numbers to Remember
- **~20-40%** of NSA IDR disputes submitted are found ineligible — don't waste filing fees
- **30 business days** — mandatory open negotiation before IDR
- **4 business days** — window to initiate IDR after negotiation ends
- **3 business days** — window to object to eligibility after IDR initiated
- **10 business days** — IDR entity confirms eligibility after selection
