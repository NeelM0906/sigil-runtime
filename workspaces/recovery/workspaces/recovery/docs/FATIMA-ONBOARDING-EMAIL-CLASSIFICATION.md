## CONTEXT

Fatima is being added to the project to provide subject matter expertise on email workflows. She is:
- Incredibly knowledgeable about email and overall workflow process
- Hesitant and uncertain since she has not worked on something like this before
- Not required to review emails individually — SAI processes first, she reviews categories

---

## THE PROCESS (As approved by Mark)

### Step 1 — Send the spreadsheet
Fatima sends a spreadsheet with two days of real emails (date, sender, subject, body).

### Step 2 — SAI processes everything first
SAI analyzes every email automatically and groups them into ~15-20 categories. Hundreds of emails become category groups. **Fatima does NOT review emails one by one.**

### Step 3 — SAI presents decisions
SAI walks through each category with examples:
- "I found 83 fee invoices from arbitrators. Proposed action: auto-trigger payment workflow. Sound right?"
- "I found 47 emails where carrier claims they never received open negotiation notice. Proposed action: auto-pull document and send standard response. Agree?"
- "I found 12 emails I'm not sure about. Can you tell me what normally happens?"

**Fatima's job:** Say yes, no, or "yes, except when X."

### Step 4 — SAI builds the rulebook
From Fatima's feedback, SAI builds a classification rulebook. Fatima reviews, corrects, signs off.

### Step 5 — Shadow mode
First week: SAI processes but doesn't send automatically. Fatima verifies. Confidence → flip the switch.

---

## FATIMA'S ESTIMATED TIME INVESTMENT
1-2 hours reviewing category decisions, not 500 individual emails.

## KEY FRAMING FOR FATIMA
- This is NOT replacing her
- This is NOT reviewing hundreds of emails individually
- There are no wrong answers
- If something doesn't feel right, that's the most important feedback
