# Callagy Recovery — Competitive Brief [July 2025]

---

## Positioning

Callagy Law's Recovery division is the only legal-first medical revenue recovery operation in the NJ/NY metro market that combines litigation-grade legal expertise with high-volume claims processing. Where competitors treat unpaid medical claims as a billing problem, Callagy treats them as a legal problem — and wins. Founded by Sean Callagy, one of only two attorneys to secure two Top 100 National Jury Verdicts while legally blind, the Recovery division carries the same relentless standard into every dollar recovered for medical providers.

---

## Competitor Landscape

| Competitor | Core Approach | Key Strength | Key Vulnerability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Degrado Law** | Legal representation for medical reimbursement (NJ/FL) | Multi-state presence across NJ and FL; established reputation in PIP/no-fault reimbursement | Smaller operation without Callagy's volume infrastructure or integrated technology stack |
| **Revenue recovery billing companies** (non-legal) | Administrative claims processing and appeals; no litigation capability | Lower cost-per-claim for straightforward denials; high-volume processing | Cannot litigate — when insurers deny or delay, these firms hit a ceiling. No courtroom leverage. |
| **Solo/boutique PIP attorneys** | Case-by-case litigation for individual medical providers | Deep personal relationships with local provider clients | Cannot scale; lack systems, technology, and team depth to handle volume across hundreds of providers simultaneously |

---

## Callagy's Competitive Advantage

- **Legal leverage at scale.** Callagy doesn't just file appeals — they file lawsuits. Insurers know this. The threat of litigation from a firm with a proven trial record changes the negotiation calculus before a case ever reaches a courtroom.

- **Proven verdict history.** Sean Callagy's two Top 100 National Jury Verdicts are not marketing — they're precedent. Carriers and adjusters recognize the name. That recognition accelerates settlements.

- **Integrated technology and process systems.** Recovery operates with systematized intake, tracking, and escalation workflows that let a legal team handle volume that would overwhelm boutique competitors. Process is the moat.

- **Dual-state knowledge (NJ + expanding).** Deep expertise in New Jersey's PIP/no-fault regulatory environment, with infrastructure designed to expand into additional states — a strategic advantage as competitors like Degrado already operate in FL.

---

## Threats to Monitor

- **Degrado Law's multi-state footprint.** Their NJ/FL dual presence gives them a geographic advantage with providers who operate across state lines. If Callagy doesn't expand strategically, Degrado could capture multi-state provider relationships first.

- **Technology-forward billing companies.** Non-legal recovery firms are investing in AI-driven claims processing and automated appeals. While they lack litigation power, their speed and cost efficiency on routine denials could erode Callagy's volume on lower-complexity claims.

- **Insurer consolidation and policy shifts.** As major carriers tighten reimbursement policies and automate denials, the total volume of disputed claims increases — but so does the sophistication required to win. Firms without deep legal infrastructure will struggle, but Callagy must stay ahead of evolving insurer tactics.

---

**Callagy Recovery doesn't compete with billing companies. It competes with law firms — and outperforms them with systems. That combination of legal firepower and operational scale is the position no competitor currently occupies.**
