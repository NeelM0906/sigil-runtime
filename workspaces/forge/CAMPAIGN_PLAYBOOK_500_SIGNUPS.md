## 1. Landing Page Patterns

### What High-Converting Enrollment Pages Look Like

The highest-converting program enrollment pages follow a predictable architecture. This isn't theory — it's the pattern used by operators like Tony Robbins/Dean Graziosi (Knowledge Business Blueprint, The Knowledge Broker Blueprint, Project Next), Mindvalley (Masterclass launches), ClickFunnels (One Funnel Away Challenge), and Alex Hormozi ($100M Leads launch).

### Page Architecture (Top to Bottom)

**Section 1: The Hook (Above the Fold)**
- **Headline:** Outcome-driven, specific, time-bound. NOT feature-driven.
  - ✅ "Join 12,000+ People Who've [Specific Outcome] in [Timeframe]"
  - ❌ "Welcome to Our Premium Program"
- **Sub-headline:** Addresses the primary objection or identifies the audience
- **Hero Video or VSL:** 2-15 minutes. Auto-plays (muted) or has a compelling thumbnail. This is the #1 conversion lever on the page.
- **Primary CTA Button:** High-contrast color, action-oriented text ("Reserve My Spot" > "Submit")
- **Trust Bar:** Logos of media features, partner brands, or "As seen in" badges

**Section 2: Problem Agitation**
- 3-5 bullet points naming the specific pains the audience experiences
- Language mirrors how the prospect describes the problem (not how the expert describes it)
- Pattern: "If you're [pain point], you're not alone. [Statistic or social proof]."

**Section 3: The Solution Framework**
- Name the methodology/framework (e.g., "The Unblinded Formula," "The CLOSER Framework")
- 3-5 pillars or modules shown visually (icons + short descriptions)
- This is NOT a full curriculum dump — it's a high-level promise architecture

**Section 4: Social Proof Stack**
- **Video testimonials** (most powerful — 60-90 seconds each, 3-6 featured)
- **Screenshot testimonials** from social media or messages
- **Results metrics:** "4,200+ graduates," "Average ROI of 3.2x," etc.
- **Before/After transformations** where applicable
- Arrangement pattern: Lead with the most relatable testimonial, not the most impressive

**Section 5: What You Get (Offer Stack)**
- List every component with perceived value
- Format: Component name → Description → Value ($X)
- Stack total value, then show the actual price
- Typical value stack ratio for premium programs: 10:1 to 20:1 (total perceived value vs. price)

**Section 6: Guarantee / Risk Reversal**
- 30-day money-back guarantee is the minimum standard
- Named guarantees convert better ("The Transformation Guarantee")
- Conditional guarantees (do the work, get results or money back) work for premium programs

**Section 7: FAQ**
- Address top 5-7 objections directly
- Common objections: time commitment, tech requirements, "will this work for me," refund policy, payment plans
- Use expandable accordion format

**Section 8: Final CTA + Urgency**
- Repeat the offer
- Add urgency: countdown timer, cohort cap, bonus deadline
- Real scarcity converts; fake scarcity damages trust long-term

### Conversion Benchmarks (Industry Knowledge)
| Traffic Source | Landing Page Conv. Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cold paid traffic | 2-5% | For webinar/challenge registration |
| Warm email list | 10-25% | For webinar/challenge registration |
| Retargeted traffic | 8-15% | People who visited but didn't convert |
| Webinar to paid program | 5-15% | End of webinar/challenge |
| Application page (high-ticket) | 20-40% | Pre-qualified applicants |

*⚠️ These are general industry benchmarks from direct-response marketing. Actual rates vary significantly by niche, price point, audience temperature, and offer quality.*

### Key Landing Page Principles
1. **One page, one goal.** No navigation menu. No distractions.
2. **Mobile-first design.** 60-70% of traffic is mobile.
3. **Page load speed matters.** Every second of delay reduces conversions ~7% (widely cited Google research).
4. **Long-form pages outperform short-form for premium offers.** The more expensive the program, the more copy is needed to justify the investment.
5. **Video placement:** Above the fold, with a compelling thumbnail showing the instructor/founder.

## 2. Email Sequences

### The Complete Email Architecture for a 90-Day Campaign

Email remains the highest-ROI channel for program launches. The architecture below maps the full journey from opt-in to enrollment.

### Phase 1: Welcome Series (Days 1-5 after opt-in)
**Purpose:** Build relationship, deliver promised value, set expectations

| Email # | Timing | Subject Line Pattern | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Immediate | "Here's your [lead magnet]" / "You're in!" | Deliver the promised value. Brief intro to who you are. Set expectation for what's coming. |
| 2 | Day 1 | "Quick question for you…" | Ask an engagement question. Share your origin story. Build connection. |
| 3 | Day 2 | "The #1 mistake [audience] makes" | Educational content. Position your framework as the solution. |
| 4 | Day 3 | "[Name], this changed everything for me" | Personal story of transformation. Introduce the methodology. |
| 5 | Day 5 | "What [successful student] did differently" | Case study/testimonial. Social proof. Soft mention of the program. |

**Key metrics to target:**
- Open rate: 40-60% (welcome series runs hot)
- Click rate: 5-15%
- Unsubscribe rate: <2% per email

### Phase 2: Nurture Series (Ongoing, 2-3x/week)
**Purpose:** Educate, build authority, segment engaged subscribers

**Content rotation (cycle through these):**
1. **Teaching emails** — Deliver genuine value. Teach one concept per email.
2. **Story emails** — Share student transformations, your own journey, behind-the-scenes
3. **Belief-shift emails** — Address limiting beliefs that prevent enrollment
4. **Engagement emails** — Polls, questions, reply-to-this prompts

**Subject line patterns that work:**
- Question format: "Have you tried this?"
- Curiosity gap: "The weird thing about [topic]…"
- Personal: "[Name], I was wrong about this"
- Direct: "3 ways to [specific outcome] this week"
- Story-based: "She was $40K in debt. Then…"

**Avoid:** ALL CAPS, excessive emojis in subject lines, misleading clickbait

### Phase 3: Launch Series (7-14 days, the enrollment window)
**Purpose:** Drive enrollment decisions

This is the highest-leverage email sequence. Structure:

| Day | Email | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| L-3 | "Something big is coming" | Tease the upcoming opening. Build anticipation. |
| L-1 | "Tomorrow changes everything" | Pre-launch excitement. Early bird hint. |
| L-Day | "Doors are open" (Email 1 of 2) | Program announcement. Full details. Early bird bonus. |
| L-Day | "Did you see this?" (Email 2) | Shorter reminder. Emphasize early bird deadline. |
| L+1 | "[Student name]'s story" | Social proof email. Testimonial-driven. |
| L+2 | "Your questions, answered" | FAQ email. Address top objections. |
| L+3 | "The real cost of waiting" | Cost-of-inaction email. What happens if nothing changes? |
| L+5 | "Behind the scenes of [Program]" | Show what's inside. Module breakdown. Instructor credibility. |
| L+7 | "Bonuses expire tonight" | Early bird deadline. Urgency with specific bonuses dropping off. |
| L+8 | "What [graduate] wishes they knew" | Another testimonial. Different angle. |
| L+10 | "48 hours left" | Cart-close sequence begins. |
| L+11 | "Final day — here's everything" | Full recap of offer, bonuses, guarantee. |
| L+11 | "Closing in 3 hours" | Final urgency email. Short, direct. |
| L+11 | "Last call" | 1 hour or "doors closing now." Plain text, personal. |

**Critical insight:** 40-60% of total sales typically come in the last 48 hours of a launch window. This is consistently observed across the industry. The cart-close sequence is NOT optional.

### Phase 4: Post-Close Nurture
- "Enrollment is closed" email (creates FOMO for next round)
- Continue nurture sequence for non-buyers
- Wait 30-60 days before the next enrollment window
- Segment: engaged-but-didn't-buy for special attention

### Email Volume Summary
- Welcome series: 5 emails
- Nurture series: 20-30 emails (ongoing)
- Launch series: 12-16 emails
- Post-close: 3-5 emails
- **Total for a 90-day campaign: 40-55 emails**

### Story-Based vs. Direct-Response Emails
Both are essential. The proven ratio:
- **Nurture phase:** 70% story/educational, 30% direct
- **Launch phase:** 40% story/testimonial, 60% direct
- **Cart close:** 90% direct, 10% story

## 3. Paid Media Strategy

### Budget Framework for 500 Signups in 90 Days

**Working backwards from the goal:**

Assumptions (adjust based on your actual funnel metrics):
- Target: 500 paid enrollments
- Webinar/challenge registration rate from ads: 20-35%
- Webinar/challenge show-up rate: 30-45%
- Webinar-to-application rate: 10-20%
- Application-to-enrollment rate: 30-50%

**Funnel math example (mid-range assumptions):**
- 500 enrollments needed
- At 10% webinar-to-sale rate → need 5,000 webinar attendees
- At 35% show-up rate → need ~14,300 registrants
- At 25% ad-to-registration rate → need ~57,000 ad clicks
- At $2-5 CPC → **Total ad budget: $114K-$285K**

*⚠️ These numbers vary enormously by niche, price point, and audience. A $997 program converts very differently from a $10,000 program. Use these as starting scaffolding, not gospel.*

**For a lower-budget approach (leveraging organic + paid):**
- Assume 50% of registrants come from organic/email/referral
- Ad budget range: $50K-$150K over 90 days
- Start with $500-1,000/day in weeks 1-4 (testing)
- Scale to $1,500-3,000/day in weeks 5-8 (proven creatives)
- Push to $2,000-5,000/day in weeks 9-12 (launch window)

### Platform-by-Platform Strategy

#### Meta (Facebook + Instagram) — Primary Channel (60-70% of budget)
**Why:** Still the most powerful platform for direct-response program enrollment. Best targeting, best retargeting, best lookalike audiences.

**Creative formats that work (in order of typical performance):**
1. **Talking-head video (60-90 seconds)** — Founder/instructor speaking directly to camera. Raw, authentic, not overproduced. Hook in first 3 seconds.
2. **UGC-style testimonial videos** — Students sharing their experience. Film on phone. Real > polished.
3. **Carousel ads** — 3-5 slides telling a story or showing transformation
4. **Image + long-form copy** — Strong headline, detailed caption (Facebook rewards engagement)
5. **Reels/Stories format** — Vertical video, 15-30 seconds, pattern-interrupt hooks

**Ad copy framework (proven structure):**
- **Hook (line 1):** Pattern interrupt or bold claim. "I enrolled 847 people in 6 weeks. Here's exactly how."
- **Problem:** Name the specific pain. "You've tried [thing] and it didn't work because…"
- **Solution:** Introduce the mechanism (not the product yet). "What I discovered is that [insight]…"
- **Proof:** Result or testimonial. "Sarah used this and [specific result]."
- **CTA:** Direct, low-commitment. "Watch the free training → [link]"

**Campaign structure:**
1. **Prospecting campaigns (cold):** Broad targeting + interest-based + lookalike audiences (1%, 3%, 5%)
2. **Engagement campaigns:** Video view audiences, page engagers
3. **Retargeting campaigns:** Website visitors, video viewers (25%, 50%, 75%, 95%), email list matches, engagement audiences
4. **Lookalike building:** Upload customer list → create 1% lookalike → test → scale winners

**Retargeting sequence (critical):**
- Day 1-3 after visit: Value-driven content (not a hard sell)
- Day 3-7: Testimonial/social proof ads
- Day 7-14: Direct offer with urgency
- Day 14-30: Softer touch, different angle, or new lead magnet
- Day 30+: Exclude or move to cold nurture

#### YouTube Ads (15-20% of budget)
**Why:** Longer attention span. People are in learning mode. Great for premium programs.

**What works:**
- **In-stream skippable ads (15-60 seconds for hook, full video 3-15 min)**
- Hook must earn the first 5 seconds (that's when they can skip)
- Teach something valuable IN the ad — don't just tease
- Target: competitor channels, relevant topic keywords, custom intent audiences
- **Discovery ads:** Thumbnail + headline, appears in search results

#### Google Search (10-15% of budget)
**Why:** Captures high-intent traffic. People actively searching for solutions.

**Keywords to target:**
- "[Problem] course/program/training"
- "[Competitor name] alternative"
- "[Topic] certification"
- "How to [specific outcome]"
- Brand name (protect your brand terms)

#### TikTok (5-10% of budget, optional)
**Why:** Lowest CPMs, youngest audience, rapidly growing for education.
**What works:** Raw, educational content. "3 things I wish I knew about [topic]." UGC-style.

### Creative Testing Framework
- Test 3-5 hooks per creative concept
- Test 2-3 thumbnail variations
- Kill underperformers at $50-100 spend (if no signal)
- Scale winners by duplicating ad sets (not increasing budget more than 20%/day)
- Refresh creatives every 2-3 weeks to combat fatigue

### Budget Allocation Across 90 Days
| Phase | Weeks | % of Budget | Daily Spend (on $150K total) | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Testing | 1-3 | 15% | ~$1,000/day | Test audiences, creatives, hooks |
| Optimization | 4-6 | 20% | ~$1,400/day | Scale winners, kill losers |
| Scaling | 7-9 | 25% | ~$1,800/day | Scale proven campaigns, build retargeting pools |
| Launch Push | 10-12 | 40% | ~$2,800/day | Maximum spend on proven winners + retargeting blitz |

## 4. Webinar / VSL / Challenge Funnels

### Comparing Launch Mechanisms

Each mechanism has different strengths. Here's the honest breakdown:

### A. Live Webinar Funnel
**How it works:** Drive traffic to a registration page → live 60-90 min presentation → pitch at the end → cart open for 5-7 days

**Pros:**
- Highest conversion rates (live creates urgency and connection)
- Real-time Q&A builds trust
- Can read the room and adjust the pitch
- Proven at massive scale (Tony Robbins, Dean Graziosi, Russell Brunson)

**Cons:**
- Requires significant promotional runway (7-14 days pre-webinar)
- Show-up rates are typically 25-40% of registrants
- One-shot — if the webinar flops, you need to regroup
- Time-zone challenges for global audiences

**Conversion benchmarks (industry knowledge):**
- Registration page: 20-40% conversion from targeted traffic
- Show-up rate: 25-40% of registrants
- Webinar-to-sale: 5-15% of attendees (for programs $500-$5,000)
- Webinar-to-application: 10-25% of attendees (for high-ticket $5,000+)

**Best for:** Established audience, strong presenter, programs $997-$10,000+

### B. Automated Webinar (Evergreen)
**How it works:** Pre-recorded webinar that runs on schedule or on-demand. Simulates live experience.

**Pros:**
- Runs 24/7 without presenter availability
- Can be optimized over time (test different versions)
- Scales infinitely
- Works across time zones

**Cons:**
- Lower conversion than live (typically 40-60% of live conversion rates)
- Audience increasingly savvy about "fake live" — authenticity matters
- Requires robust email follow-up to compensate for lower urgency

**Conversion benchmarks:**
- Similar registration rates to live
- Show-up rate: 20-35%
- Webinar-to-sale: 3-8% of attendees
- Can be improved with hybrid approaches (automated webinar + live Q&A follow-up)

**Best for:** Scaling beyond what live can handle, ongoing enrollment, time-zone coverage

### C. 5-Day Challenge Funnel ⭐ RECOMMENDED FOR 500+ ENROLLMENTS
**How it works:** Free 5-day challenge (live daily trainings, 30-60 min each) → community engagement → pitch on Day 5 → cart open for 5-7 days

**Pros:**
- Highest engagement of any launch mechanism
- Builds community before asking for money
- Multiple touchpoints = more trust than a single webinar
- Creates reciprocity through delivered value
- Strong word-of-mouth (participants share with friends)
- Multiple pitch opportunities (Day 5 main pitch + follow-up emails)
- Proven at the exact 500+ enrollment scale (ClickFunnels' "One Funnel Away Challenge" enrolled thousands per round)

**Cons:**
- More complex to execute (5 days of content + community management)
- Requires daily commitment from the team
- Higher operational overhead
- Participant drop-off across 5 days (typically 40-60% complete all 5 days)

**Conversion benchmarks:**
- Challenge registration: 25-50% from targeted traffic (higher than webinar because "free challenge" is lower commitment perception)
- Show-up rate Day 1: 40-60%
- Completion rate (all 5 days): 30-50%
- Challenge-to-sale: 8-20% of Day 1 attendees
- Challenge-to-sale: 15-30% of completers

**Best for:** Programs requiring trust-building, transformational programs, community-driven offers, the 500+ enrollment target

### D. Video Sales Letter (VSL)
**How it works:** Long-form video (15-60 min) on a sales page. No live component. Traffic → VSL page → buy now.

**Pros:**
- Always on, no scheduling needed
- Can be highly optimized (test different versions)
- Works for lower-ticket offers ($97-$497)
- Simple funnel with fewer moving parts

**Cons:**
- Lower conversion for premium programs (no live interaction)
- Requires exceptional copywriting and video production
- Harder to build community/relationship through a single video

**Conversion benchmarks:**
- VSL page conversion: 1-5% from cold traffic
- VSL page conversion: 5-15% from warm traffic
- Works best when combined with email nurture driving back to VSL

**Best for:** Lower-ticket programs, evergreen offers, supplement to other launch mechanisms

### ⚔️ Forge Recommendation: Hybrid Model

For 500+ enrollments in 90 days, use a **Challenge-First Hybrid:**

1. **Weeks 1-6:** Build audience with content, ads → lead magnet → email nurture
2. **Week 7-8:** Run a 5-Day Challenge (live)
3. **Week 8-9:** Cart open with full launch email sequence
4. **Week 9-10:** Close cart. Nurture non-buyers.
5. **Week 11:** Run an automated replay / "encore" challenge for those who missed it
6. **Week 12:** Final enrollment window with deadline

This hybrid approach gives you:
- The high conversion of live challenges
- The scale of automated replays
- Multiple enrollment windows
- Massive email list growth from challenge registrants who don't buy (future customers)

## 5. Automation & Tooling Stack

### The Minimum Viable Tech Stack

You don't need 47 tools. You need these:

### Automation Workflows to Build

**Workflow 1: Lead Capture → Nurture**
```
Opt-in → Tag as "Lead" → Welcome Series (5 emails) → Add to Nurture Sequence → Score engagement
```

**Workflow 2: Challenge Registration**
```
Challenge opt-in → Tag as "Challenge Registrant" → SMS reminder setup →

```

```
                    → No: Continue nurture
```

```
Visited checkout but didn't buy → Wait 1 hour →

```

**Workflow 5: Post-Purchase**
```
Purchase → Welcome email with login → Day 1: Onboarding video →
Day 3: Check-in email → Day 7: First milestone prompt →
Day 14: Community engagement prompt → Day 30: Testimonial request
```

## 6. Referral & Viral Mechanics

### Building Word-of-Mouth Into the Funnel

The best programs don't just buy customers — they turn customers into recruiters.

### Strategy 1: Ambassador / Affiliate Program
**How it works:** Give enrolled students a referral link. Pay them for each successful referral.

**Implementation:**
- Offer 10-20% commission per referral (standard for programs $500-$2,000)
- Or offer flat-rate bonuses ($100-$500 per referral)
- Provide pre-written copy, email templates, social media posts
- Create a private "Ambassador" channel in your community
- Leaderboard with prizes for top referrers

**Expected impact:** A well-run ambassador program can drive 10-25% of total enrollments

### Strategy 2: Bring-a-Friend Incentive
**How it works:** Current registrants/students get a bonus when they bring a friend

**Implementation for challenges:**
- "Bring 3 friends to the challenge, unlock [exclusive bonus]"
- Share-to-unlock mechanism: share a unique link → when 3 people register through it → bonus unlocks
- Bonuses: exclusive training, 1-on-1 coaching call, bonus module, discount on paid program

**Implementation for paid programs:**
- "Enroll with a friend, both get [bonus]" — e.g., extra coaching session, exclusive workshop
- Buddy pricing: Small discount when enrolling together

### Strategy 3: Challenge/Event Virality
**How it works:** Build sharing mechanics INTO the challenge experience

**Tactics:**
- **Daily share prompts:** "Share your Day 3 breakthrough on social media with #[BrandHashtag]"
- **Accountability partners:** Pair participants up → they naturally recruit friends in future rounds
- **Community engagement rewards:** Most helpful community members get recognized + prizes
- **User-generated content:** Encourage video testimonials, transformation posts
- **Certificate of completion:** Shareable social media graphic ("I completed the [Challenge Name]!")

### Strategy 4: Waitlist Virality
**How it works:** Before opening enrollment, build a waitlist with viral mechanics

**Implementation:**
- "Join the waitlist for early access"
- "Share your waitlist link — move up the list for every referral"
- Top of waitlist gets: first access, early bird pricing, exclusive bonuses
- Tools: Viral Loops, SparkLoop, or custom-built with referral tracking

**Expected impact:** Viral waitlists can 2-5x your initial list through referrals

### Strategy 5: Student Success Stories as Marketing
**How it works:** Systematically collect and amplify student results

**Implementation:**
- **Day 30 survey:** Automated email requesting results and testimonial
- **Quarterly showcase:** Feature top student transformations in email, ads, social
- **Video testimonial collection:** Send students a simple prompt + recording instructions (or use VideoAsk)
- **Success story blog posts / podcast episodes:** Deep-dive interviews with graduates
- Use these in ALL marketing: ads, emails, landing pages, social proof

### Strategy 6: Community-Driven Enrollment
**How it works:** Make the community itself a selling point

**Implementation:**
- Run a public-facing community (Skool, Facebook Group) with free members
- Deliver real value in the free community
- Paid program members get access to premium channels/features
- Free members see the transformations of paid members
- Natural migration from free → paid

## 7. 90-Day Implementation Timeline

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-3)
- [ ] Define the offer: pricing, bonuses, guarantee, payment plans
- [ ] Build landing pages: lead magnet page, challenge registration page, sales page
- [ ] Set up tech stack: CRM, email platform, payment processor, analytics
- [ ] Create lead magnet (free resource that attracts ideal prospects)
- [ ] Write welcome email series (5 emails)
- [ ] Install tracking pixels (Meta, Google, analytics)
- [ ] Record 3-5 ad creatives (talking head + testimonials)
- [ ] Set up retargeting audiences
- [ ] Build the community space (Circle, Skool, or Facebook Group)

### Phase 2: Audience Building (Weeks 4-6)
- [ ] Launch paid ads to lead magnet (testing phase: 3-5 audiences, 3-5 creatives)
- [ ] Begin organic content strategy (3-5 posts/week on primary platform)
- [ ] Start nurture email sequence for new leads
- [ ] Optimize ads: kill losers, scale winners
- [ ] Begin challenge/webinar content preparation
- [ ] Recruit 5-10 beta testers / early success stories for testimonials
- [ ] Build lookalike audiences from initial leads
- [ ] **Target:** 2,000-5,000 email subscribers by end of Week 6

### Phase 3: Challenge / Launch Event (Weeks 7-9)
- [ ] Open challenge registration (2 weeks before start)
- [ ] Email blast to full list announcing the challenge
- [ ] Run dedicated ads to challenge registration page
- [ ] Activate bring-a-friend viral mechanics
- [ ] Run 5-Day Challenge (live daily trainings)
- [ ] Daily email reminders + SMS reminders
- [ ] Community engagement: daily prompts, Q&A, prizes
- [ ] Day 5: Make the pitch. Open cart.
- [ ] Launch email sequence (12-16 emails over 7-10 days)
- [ ] Cart-close sequence (final 48 hours)
- [ ] **Target:** 300-400 enrollments from primary challenge

### Phase 4: Scale & Close (Weeks 10-12)
- [ ] Nurture non-buyers from the challenge
- [ ] Run automated challenge replay / encore
- [ ] Retargeting ad blitz on challenge attendees who didn't buy
- [ ] Application calls for undecided prospects (if high-ticket)
- [ ] Activate ambassador program for enrolled students
- [ ] Second enrollment window with new deadline
- [ ] Final cart close with hard deadline
- [ ] Post-campaign: collect testimonials, analyze data, document learnings
- [ ] **Target:** 100-200 additional enrollments from encore + referrals

## 8. Key Metrics Dashboard

### Track These Numbers Weekly

**Top of Funnel:**
- Cost per lead (CPL) — target: $2-10 for challenge registrations
- Lead volume per day
- Ad spend / ROAS
- Landing page conversion rate

**Middle of Funnel:**
- Email open rate (target: 25-40% for nurture, 40-60% for launch)
- Email click rate (target: 3-8%)
- Challenge registration rate
- Challenge show-up rate (Day 1)
- Challenge completion rate (all 5 days)
- Webinar attendance rate

**Bottom of Funnel:**
- Sales page conversion rate
- Cart abandonment rate
- Application-to-enrollment rate
- Revenue per lead
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Lifetime value (LTV) if known

**Post-Sale:**
- Refund rate (target: <10% for well-positioned offers)
- Net Promoter Score
- Testimonial collection rate
- Referral rate per customer

### Health Indicators
- ✅ **Healthy:** CPL < $8, email open > 30%, challenge show-up > 40%, refund < 8%
- ⚠️ **Watch:** CPL $8-15, email open 20-30%, show-up 25-40%, refund 8-15%
- 🔴 **Fix Immediately:** CPL > $15, email open < 20%, show-up < 25%, refund > 15%

## Appendix: What NOT to Do

1. **Don't launch without an email list.** Paid ads alone won't get you to 500. Build the list FIRST (weeks 1-6).
2. **Don't skip the cart-close sequence.** Leaving money on the table. Most sales come in the last 48 hours.
3. **Don't use fake urgency.** "Only 7 spots left" when there's no limit. Audiences are savvy. Use real deadlines (cohort start dates, bonus deadlines).
4. **Don't over-produce ad creatives.** Raw, authentic video outperforms polished corporate content for direct-response.
5. **Don't price without payment plans.** Offering 3-6 monthly payments can increase conversions 30-50% for programs over $500.
6. **Don't ignore mobile.** Test every page, every email on mobile first.
7. **Don't blast your entire list with the same message.** Segment by engagement level, challenge attendance, page visits.
8. **Don't neglect post-purchase onboarding.** Buyer's remorse drives refunds. The first 48 hours after purchase are critical.
9. **Don't run a challenge without a community.** The community IS the transformation mechanism. No community = no stickiness = no word-of-mouth.
10. **Don't expect 500 enrollments on your first try without iteration.** Most successful programs refined their funnel over 2-3 launches.
