## Core methodology (what Sean is pointing at)
1) **Competitive scoreboards / leaderboards** as a primary behavior driver.
   - Anonymous or named peer comparisons after assessments.
   - Vertical-specific: lawyers, accountants, financial services providers.

2) **Split-testing at massive scale**
   - Multiple simultaneous tests: **A vs B, B vs C, C vs D, even AAA vs DDB**.
   - Claim: “We get to in a day what digital markets would take a year to never get to.”

3) **A/B testing must be grounded in *actual humans***
   - Copy/SimHumans written from **real LinkedIn bios**, not generic templates.
   - Purpose: the AI can **properly acknowledge the person** in the phone call / conversation.

4) **LinkedIn bio data integration**
   - Genesis Forge has a “bio feature” where contact info is shared with AI (Milo) so it can acknowledge.
   - Perplexity used to scrape LinkedIn profiles.
   - Nick Roy created a Perplexity prompt for bio extraction.
   - Reported: **100,000+ lawyer contacts** compiled with LinkedIn info (Google Sheet).

5) **Dashboards + gamification**
   - Scoreboards “everywhere” (Texas/Dallas examples) to move people up org/school boards.
   - “Come Get Me” project: 12 sections inside Colosseum architecture; Battle Arena V3; side memory; 30+ hours continuous operation.
